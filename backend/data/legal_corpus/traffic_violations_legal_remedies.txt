TRAFFIC VIOLATIONS — LEGAL REMEDIES AND COMPLAINT PROCEDURES
Comprehensive Guide for Indian Citizens
(Based on Motor Vehicles Act 1988, Code of Criminal Procedure, Consumer Protection Act, RTI Act)

## PART A — HOW TO CONTEST A TRAFFIC CHALLAN

### Step 1: Understanding Your Challan
A traffic challan (notice of violation) can be issued:
(a) On-the-spot by a traffic police officer
(b) Electronically (e-challan) based on camera evidence
(c) By post, based on CCTV/speed camera/ANPR evidence

### Step 2: Rights of the Person Challaned
You have the right to:
1. Know the specific offence and section of law violated
2. See the evidence (photograph, video, breathalyser reading)
3. Ask for the police officer's name, badge number, and posting
4. Get a receipt for any fine paid
5. Contest the challan in court if you disagree
6. Not be physically detained for compoundable offences (only documentation required)
7. Pay the fine online without visiting the court (for compoundable offences)
8. Request inspection of electronic evidence in e-challan cases

### Step 3: Contesting a Challan
Option 1 — Challenge before the Court
(a) Do not pay the challan fine (payment is treated as admission of guilt under compounding)
(b) Appear before the magistrate court on the date mentioned in the challan
(c) Present your defence with evidence (dashcam footage, GPS records, witness statements, photographs)
(d) If the challan date is already passed, file an application for hearing
(e) Court will hear both sides and decide
(f) If acquitted, no fine payable and endorsement removed from record

Option 2 — Online Grievance/Challenge
(a) Many states now allow online challenge of e-challans
(b) Upload counter-evidence on the e-challan portal
(c) Designated officer reviews and decides
(d) If challenge accepted, challan is cancelled

Option 3 — Approach Higher Officers
(a) File complaint with the DCP Traffic / Commissioner of Police
(b) Provide evidence of wrongful challan
(c) Request review and cancellation

### Step 4: Common Grounds for Contesting Challans
1. Wrong vehicle challaned (wrong number plate reading by camera)
2. Vehicle was not at the location (GPS/toll records as proof)
3. No violation committed (dashcam footage as evidence)
4. Challan issued without jurisdiction
5. E-challan photo is unclear or shows different vehicle
6. Duplicate challan for same offence
7. Challan issued after time limit (varies by state)
8. Officer did not follow proper procedure
9. Electronic device not calibrated/certified (speed cameras, breathalysers)

## PART B — COMPLAINTS AGAINST TRAFFIC POLICE

### Grounds for Complaint
1. Demanding bribe / illegal gratification
2. Rude / abusive behaviour
3. Issuing false challan
4. Harassment without valid reason
5. Not following proper procedure (not wearing name plate, not showing ID)
6. Impounding vehicle without valid reason
7. Not returning documents within specified time
8. Physical violence or threat
9. Discriminatory behaviour

### Where to File Complaint

#### 1. Police Internal Complaints
- File complaint with SP/SSP/Commissioner of Police
- Written complaint to DCP Traffic
- Complaint at police control room (dial 100 or 112)
- Online complaint on state police website

#### 2. Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB)
- If officer demands bribe
- Complaint to state ACB / Vigilance Department
- Can approach CBI for central government employees

#### 3. State Human Rights Commission
- For cases of police excess / human rights violation
- Written complaint with details and evidence

#### 4. Court
- Criminal complaint under Section 166, 167, 409 IPC for wrongful detention
- Writ petition in High Court for fundamental rights violation
- Section 197 CrPC — sanction required for prosecution of public servant

#### 5. RTI Application
- File RTI to get details of the challan, procedure followed, calibration certificates of equipment
- RTI fee: Rs 10 (postal order / online)
- Response: Within 30 days
- First appeal: Within 30 days of receiving response
- Second appeal: To State/Central Information Commission

### How to File an Effective Complaint
1. Record the incident (video/audio if possible — legal in public places)
2. Note officer's name, badge number, vehicle number, location, time
3. Get witness contact details
4. File complaint promptly (within 24-48 hours)
5. Keep copies of all documents
6. Follow up regularly
7. Escalate if no response within 30 days

## PART C — LEGAL REMEDIES FOR COMMON TRAFFIC ISSUES

### 1. Wrongful Vehicle Impounding
Rights:
- Vehicle can only be impounded under specific sections (192, 194, 207)
- Officer must give receipt for impounded vehicle
- Vehicle should not be damaged while in custody
- If documents produced within 15 days, vehicle must be released

Remedy:
- Apply to officer who impounded, with valid documents
- If refused: Apply to higher officer / court
- Writ petition in High Court if illegal impounding
- Claim for damage to vehicle during impounding (civil suit / consumer court)

### 2. Towing of Parked Vehicle
Rules:
- Towing only permitted from no-parking zones or tow-away zones
- Notice/signage must be present at the location
- Towing charge is fixed by local authority (typically Rs 500-2,000 depending on city and vehicle type)
- Vehicle must not be damaged during towing
- Storage charges per day (typically Rs 100-500)

Rights:
- You can contest the towing if no-parking sign was absent
- You can claim damages for any harm to vehicle during towing
- Towing without proper signage is illegal

Remedy:
- Approach traffic police for release with payment of towing and parking charges
- Contest in traffic court if you believe towing was unjustified
- Consumer court for damage to vehicle during towing
- File complaint with municipal corporation if no signage

### 3. Road Accident — What to Do (Victim's Guide)
Immediate steps:
1. Call 112 (emergency number) or 108 (ambulance)
2. Seek medical help — any hospital must treat (Section 134A, Good Samaritan protection)
3. File FIR at nearest police station (you or a relative can file)
4. Inform insurance company

If driver fled (hit and run):
1. Note vehicle number, colour, type if possible
2. Check for CCTV cameras nearby
3. File FIR mentioning hit and run
4. Apply for hit-and-run compensation under Section 161:
   - Rs 2,00,000 for death
   - Rs 50,000 for grievous hurt
5. Application to Claims Enquiry Officer (appointed by District Collector)
6. Time limit: 6 months (extendable for sufficient cause)

Compensation claim:
1. File claim before Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT)
2. No court fee required
3. Can hire lawyer or file personally
4. Compensation based on:
   - Age and income of victim
   - Multiplier method (as per Supreme Court guidelines in Sarla Verma v. Delhi Transport Corporation)
   - Medical expenses (past and future)
   - Loss of income
   - Pain and suffering
   - Loss of earning capacity (for injury cases)
   - Consortium and love/affection (for death cases)

### 4. Insurance Claim Disputes
If insurance company rejects claim:
1. First approach: Insurance company grievance cell
2. Second approach: Insurance Ombudsman
   - Jurisdiction: Claims up to Rs 30 lakhs
   - Free of cost
   - Decision within 3 months
3. Third approach: Consumer Dispute Redressal Commission
   - District Commission: Up to Rs 1 crore
   - State Commission: Rs 1 crore to Rs 10 crores
   - National Commission: Above Rs 10 crores
4. Fourth approach: MACT (for third-party claims)
5. Civil Court (rare, usually MACT preferred)

Common rejection grounds and remedies:
- "Driver didn't have valid licence": If licence was valid but with minor defect (e.g., expired by few days), courts have held insurer cannot reject
- "Policy lapsed": Check if within grace period; challenge if renewal was in process
- "Vehicle used for commercial purpose on personal policy": Ensure correct policy type
- "Intoxication": Insurer must prove connection between intoxication and accident
- "Consequential loss": May not be covered — check policy terms
- "Delayed intimation": Courts have been lenient if delay is reasonable and explained

### 5. Rash and Negligent Driving by Others
Criminal remedy:
- FIR under Section 279 IPC (rash driving) and Section 338 IPC (causing grievous hurt)
- Section 304A IPC (causing death by negligence)
- Section 184A MV Act (new provision for causing death by driving negligence — up to 7 years imprisonment)

Civil remedy:
- MACT claim for compensation
- Civil suit for damages

### 6. Noise Pollution from Vehicles
Relevant law:
- Rule 95 CMVR (noise limits)
- Environment Protection Act, 1986
- Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000

Complaint:
- Complaint to traffic police about vehicles with modified silencers
- Complaint to PCB (Pollution Control Board) about noise pollution
- Court complaint if persistent issue
- Fine: Up to Rs 10,000 for modified exhaust/silencer

### 7. Illegal Parking by Others Blocking Your Property/Road
Remedy:
- Call traffic helpline (varies by city)
- File complaint on traffic police app
- Complaint to local police station
- Towing request through traffic police
- If persistent: Complaint to municipal corporation
- Court order in extreme cases (nuisance suit)

## PART D — CONSUMER PROTECTION FOR VEHICLE BUYERS

### 1. Defective Vehicle
Under Consumer Protection Act, 2019:
- File complaint with District Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission
- Claim: Repair, replacement, or refund
- Time limit: 2 years from date of defect discovery
- No advocate fee required (can argue personally)
- Evidence: Service records, photographs, expert opinion

Under Sale of Goods Act:
- Implied warranty of merchantability and fitness
- Right to reject goods within reasonable time
- Right to damages

### 2. Dealer / Manufacturer Negligence
- Failure to complete registration: Consumer complaint + RTO complaint
- Overcharging: Complaint to Consumer Commission + manufacturer complaint
- Warranty denial: Consumer complaint with service records
- Fraudulent accessories: Consumer complaint + police complaint

### 3. Insurance Company Unfair Practices
- Rejecting valid claims: Insurance Ombudsman + IRDAI complaint
- Delaying settlement: Complaint to IRDAI
- Unfair premium charges: IRDAI
- Non-issuance of policy: Consumer complaint

## PART E — RTI FOR TRAFFIC-RELATED INFORMATION

### What Information Can Be Sought
1. E-challan details and evidence (photographs, video)
2. Speed camera calibration certificates
3. Breathalyser calibration certificates
4. Traffic signal timing at specific intersections
5. Road accident statistics
6. RTO/transport department records
7. Vehicle registration details (public information)
8. Traffic police deployment details
9. Revenue collection from challans
10. Road condition reports and repair schedules

### How to File RTI
1. Online: RTI portal (rtionline.gov.in) for central departments
2. Offline: Written application on plain paper to PIO (Public Information Officer) of relevant department
3. Fee: Rs 10 (postal order / DD / online payment)
4. BPL applicants: No fee (attach BPL card copy)
5. Response: Within 30 days (48 hours if relating to life/liberty)

### First Appeal
- If no response within 30 days or unsatisfactory response
- Appeal to First Appellate Authority (senior officer in same department)
- Within 30 days of receiving response (or 30 days after expected response date)
- No additional fee

### Second Appeal
- If first appeal unsatisfactory
- Appeal to State/Central Information Commission
- Within 90 days of first appeal decision
- No fee
- Commission can impose penalty on PIO for non-compliance

## PART G — TIMELINE FOR LEGAL REMEDIES

Action | Time Limit
Contest challan in court | Appear on date mentioned or within 60 days
File FIR for accident | As soon as possible (no strict limit, but delay explained)
MACT claim | Within limitation period (effectively no rigid limit per Supreme Court; generally 2 years)
Consumer complaint | Within 2 years from cause of action
Insurance claim intimation | As per policy (typically 24-48 hours for accident; 24 hours for theft)
RTI application response | 30 days
First RTI appeal | 30 days from response / non-response
Hit-and-run compensation | 6 months from accident (extendable)
Licence suspension appeal | 30 days from order
Vehicle release after impounding | Documents to be produced within 15 days
