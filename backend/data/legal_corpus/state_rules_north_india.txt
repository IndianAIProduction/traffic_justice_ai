STATE-SPECIFIC RTO RULES — NORTH INDIA
Covers: Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Chandigarh, Jammu & Kashmir, Ladakh

## DELHI (National Capital Territory)

### Delhi Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1962
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 4% of vehicle cost (one-time)
- Cars up to Rs 6 lakhs: 4% (one-time)
- Cars Rs 6-10 lakhs: 7%
- Cars above Rs 10 lakhs: 10%
- Diesel vehicles: Additional 2.5% environment surcharge
- CNG vehicles: 25% discount on road tax
- Electric vehicles: 100% road tax exemption (Delhi EV Policy 2020)

### Delhi EV Policy 2020
- Road tax exemption for battery EVs
- Registration fee waiver
- Purchase incentive: Up to Rs 30,000 for two-wheelers, up to Rs 1.5 lakhs for four-wheelers
- Scrapping incentive for replacing old vehicles with EVs
- Charging infrastructure: Public charging stations mandated in new buildings

### Delhi Traffic Rules (Specific)
(a) Odd-even scheme: May be activated during high pollution (odd/even number plates on alternate days)
(b) Red light on, engine off: Campaign for reducing idle pollution at traffic signals
(c) No entry for commercial vehicles (heavy): 7:00 AM to 11:00 PM on certain roads
(d) RFID tags for commercial vehicles entering Delhi
(e) Green tax: For vehicles older than 15 years (additional 25-50% of road tax)
(f) No diesel vehicles older than 10 years in Delhi-NCR (Supreme Court/NGT order)
(g) No petrol vehicles older than 15 years in Delhi-NCR
(h) Congestion charge: Under consideration for commercial areas

### Delhi Fine Structure (E-Challan)
Delhi follows central fine amounts as per 2019 MV Act amendment.
E-challan system: Fully electronic, cameras at major intersections.
Payment: Online via delhitrafficpolice.nic.in or Parivahan portal
Challenge: Online challenge available on e-challan portal

### Delhi RTO Offices
Main RTO: Sarai Kale Khan, IP Estate
Sub-RTOs: Janakpuri, Rohini, Loni Road, Surajmal Vihar, Vasant Vihar
Website: transport.delhi.gov.in
Helpline: 1095

## UTTAR PRADESH

### UP Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1997
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers up to 100cc: 4% of vehicle cost (one-time)
- Two-wheelers above 100cc: 6%
- Cars up to 1000cc: 6%
- Cars 1000-1500cc: 7%
- Cars above 1500cc: 8%
- SUVs/Luxury cars: 9-10%
- Diesel surcharge: 2% additional
- Electric vehicles: Exempted until 2025 (UP EV Policy 2022)

### UP-Specific Traffic Rules
(a) UP adopts central fine schedule with some state modifications
(b) E-challan active in: Lucknow, Noida, Agra, Varanasi, Kanpur, Prayagraj, Ghaziabad, Meerut
(c) Speed cameras: Deployed on Agra-Lucknow Expressway, Purvanchal Expressway, Yamuna Expressway
(d) Commercial vehicle entry restrictions: Apply in major cities during peak hours
(e) Heavy vehicles: Restricted on city roads during 8 AM to 10 PM in major cities
(f) Fitness testing centres: Being established in all districts

### UP Transport Corporation (UPSRTC)
- State bus service covering all 75 districts
- Online booking: upsrtc.com
- Concessional passes for students, senior citizens, freedom fighters

### UP RTO Network
75 RTOs (one per district)
Main RTO: Lucknow
Website: uptransport.gov.in
Helpline: 0522-2638933

## UTTARAKHAND

### Uttarakhand Motor Vehicles Taxation Rules
Road tax:
- Two-wheelers: 4.5% (one-time)
- Cars up to 1200cc: 5.5%
- Cars above 1200cc: 6.5%
- SUVs/Luxury: 7-8%

### Uttarakhand-Specific Rules
(a) Uttarakhand reduced many central fine amounts after 2019 amendment:
    - General offence (Section 177): Rs 500 → Rs 100 for first, Rs 300 for repeat
    - No helmet (Section 194B): Rs 1,000 → Rs 500 first, Rs 1,000 repeat
    - Mobile phone use (Section 194D): Rs 5,000 → Rs 1,000 first, Rs 2,000 repeat
(b) Hill road special rules:
    - Uphill traffic has right of way over downhill traffic
    - Honking mandatory on blind curves
    - Chain driving may be required in snow areas
    - Headlights mandatory in tunnels
(c) Eco-sensitive zones: Speed restrictions near Corbett, Rajaji National Park
(d) Tourist vehicles: Special permits required for certain areas (Char Dham route)

### Uttarakhand RTO
Main RTO: Dehradun
Website: transport.uk.gov.in
Districts: 13 RTOs

## HIMACHAL PRADESH

### HP Motor Vehicles Taxation Act
Road tax:
- Two-wheelers: 3-5%
- Cars: 5-8% based on engine capacity
- Electric vehicles: Reduced rates

### HP-Specific Rules
(a) HP also reduced some central fines:
    - No helmet: Rs 1,000 → Rs 500
    - General offence: Rs 500 → Rs 200
(b) Hill road regulations:
    - Green card/permit required for certain routes (Rohtang Pass, Manali-Leh)
    - Inner Line Permit for border areas (Spiti, Kinnaur)
    - Vehicle fitness strictly checked at checkpoints
    - Snow chains mandatory in winter on high-altitude routes
(c) Tourist taxi permits: Special scheme for HP-registered tourist taxis
(d) Pollution check mandatory at entry points

### HP Transport Corporation (HRTC)
- Extensive network in hilly terrain
- Online booking: hrtchp.com
- Special services: Volvo, semi-luxury for tourist routes

### HP RTO
Main RTO: Shimla
Website: himachal.nic.in/transport
Districts: 12 RTOs

## PUNJAB

### Punjab Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1924
Road tax:
- Two-wheelers: 6% (one-time)
- Cars up to 1000cc: 6%
- Cars 1000-2000cc: 7%
- Cars above 2000cc: 8%
- Diesel vehicles: Additional surcharge
- Electric vehicles: Exempted (Punjab EV Policy)

### Punjab-Specific Rules
(a) Punjab follows central fine schedule largely
(b) E-challan active in: Chandigarh (UT), Ludhiana, Amritsar, Jalandhar
(c) Sikh turbaned persons: Helmet exemption fully applicable
(d) Agricultural vehicles: Tractor registration and road tax rules (special provisions)
(e) Goods vehicles: State-specific goods tax at entry points being replaced by e-way bill
(f) Sand mining vehicles: Special permits and route restrictions

### Punjab Roadways (PRTC/PUNBUS)
- State bus service
- PUNBUS and PRTC (two corporations)
- Online booking available
- Student concession: 50% on bus pass

### Punjab RTO
Main RTO: Chandigarh (shared with Haryana)
Website: punjabtransport.org
Districts: 22+ RTOs

## HARYANA

### Haryana Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 2016
Road tax:
- Two-wheelers: 5-7%
- Cars up to 1000cc: 5%
- Cars 1000-2000cc: 6%
- Cars above 2000cc: 8%
- SUVs above Rs 20 lakhs: 10%
- Electric vehicles: Exempted

### Haryana-Specific Rules
(a) Haryana follows central fine schedule
(b) E-challan: Active in Gurugram, Faridabad, Panchkula, Ambala, Karnal
(c) KMP Expressway: FASTag mandatory, speed limit 120 km/h
(d) Delhi-NCR restrictions apply to Gurugram, Faridabad (NCR areas)
(e) Vehicle scrapping: Early adoption of vehicle scrapping policy
(f) Green tax: Applicable for vehicles above 15 years

### Haryana Roadways
- Extensive network
- Volvo and semi-luxury services to Delhi, Punjab, Rajasthan
- Online booking: hartrans.gov.in

### Haryana RTO
Main RTO: Chandigarh (shared with Punjab)
Website: hartrans.gov.in
Districts: 22+ RTOs

## CHANDIGARH (Union Territory)

### Registration and Tax
- Separate registration code (CH)
- Road tax rates similar to Punjab/Haryana
- E-challan fully operational
- HSRP mandatory for all vehicles
- BH series registration available

### Chandigarh-Specific Rules
(a) Stricter enforcement compared to neighbouring states
(b) Full central fine rates applicable
(c) No heavy commercial vehicles in residential sectors
(d) Parking: Multi-level parking systems in sectors
(e) Bicycle-friendly infrastructure
(f) CCTV-based enforcement at all major intersections

## JAMMU & KASHMIR

### J&K Motor Vehicles Rules
(a) Separate taxation structure
(b) Road tax: Generally lower than mainland states (4-6% for cars)
(c) Tourist vehicles: Special permits required
(d) Security check: Vehicles checked at multiple points
(e) Highway restrictions: Timing restrictions on Jammu-Srinagar highway (convoy system)
(f) E-challan: Being introduced

### J&K Specific Provisions
(a) Inner Line Permit not required after Article 370 abrogation
(b) National Highway timing: Specific days/times for one-way traffic on NH44
(c) Snow chain requirements: Mandatory in winter months
(d) Military vehicle priority: On certain routes during operations
(e) Tourist registration: At checkpoints for certain areas

## LADAKH (Union Territory)

### Ladakh Transport Rules
(a) Separate UT since 2019
(b) Inner Line Permit: Required for foreign nationals in certain areas
(c) Eco-fee: For vehicles entering Ladakh
(d) Altitude sickness advisory: For drivers at high altitude
(e) Road condition: Many roads seasonal (May-October)
(f) BRO (Border Roads Organisation): Maintains most roads
(g) Fuel availability: Limited beyond Leh — plan accordingly
(h) Speed limit: Very low on mountain roads (20-40 km/h)
