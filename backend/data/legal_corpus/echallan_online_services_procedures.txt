E-CHALLAN, ONLINE SERVICES AND DIGITAL PROCEDURES — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
(Based on Motor Vehicles Act 2019 Amendment, IT Act, and MoRTH Guidelines)

## PART A — E-CHALLAN SYSTEM

### What is E-Challan
Electronic traffic challan (notice of violation) issued through digital means:
(a) CCTV camera-detected violations
(b) Speed camera violations
(c) ANPR (Automatic Number Plate Recognition) violations
(d) Traffic police using handheld devices / body cameras
(e) Red light violation cameras

### How E-Challan Works
1. Violation detected: By camera system or traffic police officer with handheld device
2. Vehicle identified: Through ANPR (number plate recognition)
3. Owner identified: Through Vahan database (registration records)
4. E-challan generated: Digital notice created with violation details, photo evidence
5. Notice sent: Via SMS, email, and/or post to registered owner
6. Owner pays fine: Online or at designated centres
7. Non-payment: Vehicle flagged in Vahan system; may be impounded during next check

### E-Challan Payment Methods
1. State e-challan portals:
   - Delhi: delhitrafficpolice.nic.in → e-challan → enter vehicle number
   - Maharashtra: mahatrafficechallan.gov.in
   - Karnataka: echallan.karnataka.gov.in
   - Tamil Nadu: echallan.parivahan.gov.in/tnp
   - Telangana: echallan.tspolice.gov.in
   - UP: echallan.parivahan.gov.in/upp
   - Gujarat: echallan.parivahan.gov.in/gjp
   - Rajasthan: echallan.parivahan.gov.in/rjp

2. National Parivahan portal: echallan.parivahan.gov.in (covers multiple states)

3. Payment options:
   - Debit/Credit card
   - Net banking
   - UPI (Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm)
   - Digital wallets
   - At post offices (select states)
   - At designated bank branches

### How to Check E-Challan Status
1. Visit state e-challan portal or echallan.parivahan.gov.in
2. Enter vehicle registration number (e.g., MH01AB1234)
3. Enter chassis number (last 5 digits) or DL number for verification
4. View all pending and paid challans
5. Download/print challan receipt after payment

### How to Contest E-Challan
Step 1: View the evidence
- Login to e-challan portal
- View photograph/video evidence of violation
- Note challan number, date, time, location, section of law

Step 2: Online challenge (where available)
- Click "Contest" or "Challenge" on the e-challan portal
- Upload counter-evidence (dashcam footage, GPS records, etc.)
- Write explanation
- Submit for review
- Decision by designated officer within 30 days

Step 3: Court challenge
- If online challenge is rejected or not available
- Appear before the traffic magistrate court
- Present evidence and defence
- Court decides on merits

Step 4: Higher appeal
- If magistrate upholds challan, appeal to Sessions Court
- Further appeal to High Court (rare for traffic challans)

### Common E-Challan Issues and Solutions
Problem: Challan issued for wrong vehicle (ANPR misread)
Solution: Contest online with proof of vehicle identity (RC copy, photographs)

Problem: Vehicle was not at the location (sold/transferred)
Solution: Upload Form 29/30 proof; NOC from buyer; inform RTO of transfer

Problem: Duplicate challan for same offence
Solution: Screenshot both challans; contest the duplicate

Problem: Photo evidence is unclear
Solution: Challenge on grounds of insufficient evidence

Problem: E-challan sent to old address
Solution: Update address in Vahan database; track challans online by vehicle number

## PART C — mPARIVAHAN APP

### Features
1. Virtual DL: Digital copy of driving licence (legally valid for verification)
2. Virtual RC: Digital copy of registration certificate
3. Challan status: Check and pay pending challans
4. Vehicle information: Search any vehicle by registration number

6. PUC status: Check PUC certificate status
7. FASTag information: Linked FASTag details

### How to Use
1. Download from Google Play Store or Apple App Store
2. Register with mobile number (linked to DL/RC)
3. Link your DL and RC using Aadhaar authentication or OTP
4. Virtual documents available immediately
5. Show virtual DL/RC during police verification

- Digital DL and RC on mParivahan are legally valid as per IT Act, 2000
- Police officers are trained to accept digital documents
- Cannot be refused for not having physical copy if digital copy is available
- DigiLocker documents also legally valid

## PART E — FASTTAG (ELECTRONIC TOLL COLLECTION)

### What is FASTag
RFID-based toll collection system for national and state highways
Mandatory for all four-wheelers (since February 2021)

### How it Works
1. RFID tag affixed to vehicle windshield
2. At toll plaza, sensor reads tag
3. Toll amount deducted from linked prepaid wallet or bank account
4. Receipt sent via SMS
5. No stopping required (can pass through dedicated FASTag lane at toll speed)

### Where to Get FASTag
1. Banks: SBI, ICICI, HDFC, Axis, Paytm Payments Bank, etc.
2. Online: Amazon, Flipkart, Paytm, bank websites
3. Physical: Toll plazas, bank branches, Point of Sale (POS) agents
4. Cost: Rs 100-500 (tag cost) + initial recharge amount

### FASTag Recharge
1. Through issuing bank's app/website
2. UPI payment to FASTag wallet
3. Net banking
4. Paytm, PhonePe, Google Pay

### FASTag Penalties
- No FASTag: Double toll charged at toll plaza
- Insufficient balance: Double toll OR denied entry
- Blacklisted FASTag: Due to non-payment; must resolve with bank

### FASTag for Different Vehicle Categories
Category | Description
Car/Jeep/Van (4+1) | Light passenger vehicle
LCV (2 axle) | Light commercial vehicle
Bus/Truck (3 axle) | Medium goods/passenger
MAV (4-6 axle) | Heavy multi-axle vehicle
Oversized vehicle (7+ axle) | Heavy construction/mining vehicle

### Common FASTag Issues
Problem: Double deduction
Solution: Check transaction history on bank app; raise dispute with bank within 10 days

Problem: FASTag not reading
Solution: Check tag placement (should be behind rear-view mirror on windshield); ensure no metallic tint blocking signal

Problem: Wrong vehicle category charged
Solution: Raise complaint on NHAI website (nhai.gov.in); call NHAI helpline 1033

Problem: FASTag linked to wrong vehicle
Solution: Contact issuing bank; update vehicle details

## PART G — RTI FOR TRAFFIC AND TRANSPORT MATTERS

### How to File RTI for Traffic Matters

Online Method:
1. Visit rtionline.gov.in (for central government departments)
2. Or visit state RTI portal (for state transport departments)
3. Register and login
4. Select department: Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (for central) OR State Transport Department
5. Write your query clearly
6. Pay Rs 10 online
7. Submit

Offline Method:
1. Write application on plain paper addressed to PIO of relevant department
2. Clearly state: "Under Right to Information Act, 2005, I seek the following information..."
3. List your questions specifically
4. Attach Rs 10 postal order or DD
5. Send by registered post or deliver in person
6. Keep receipt/proof of delivery

### What You Can Ask For
1. E-challan evidence (photographs, videos) — "Please provide the photographic evidence for e-challan number XXXX dated XX/XX/XXXX"
2. Speed camera calibration — "Please provide the last calibration certificate of speed camera at [location]"
3. Breathalyser calibration — "Please provide calibration records of breathalyser used on [date] at [location]"
4. Traffic signal timing — "Please provide the signal timing (green/red/yellow) at [intersection]"
5. Road accident data — "Please provide road accident statistics for [area/road] for [period]"
6. Fine collection data — "Please provide total fine collection from e-challans in [area] for [period]"
7. RTO application status — "Please provide the status of my application number [XXXX]"
8. Vehicle registration details — "Please provide the registered owner details of vehicle [number]" (may be denied for privacy)

### RTI Timeline
- Response: Within 30 days
- Life/liberty matters: Within 48 hours
- First appeal: Within 30 days of response
- Second appeal: Within 90 days of first appeal decision

### RTI Tips
1. Be specific in your questions
2. Ask for documents/records, not opinions
3. One topic per RTI application (better response)
4. Keep copies of all applications and responses
5. Use RTI as leverage to get information for challan challenge
6. RTI is powerful for exposing faulty equipment or improper procedures

## PART I — DIGITAL COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

### Mandatory Digital Requirements (Post-2019 Amendment)
1. HSRP (High Security Registration Plate): All vehicles
2. FASTag: All four-wheelers using toll roads
3. GPS/tracking: All commercial vehicles with national permit
4. Speed governor: All transport vehicles (connected to digital monitoring)
5. Vahan registration: All new vehicles registered through Vahan
6. Sarathi DL: All new DLs through Sarathi portal
7. Electronic fitness testing: Being rolled out for transport vehicles
8. E-way bill: For goods transport (GST requirement; Rs 50,000+ value)

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- No HSRP: Fine up to Rs 5,000 (Section 192)
- No FASTag: Double toll charged
- No GPS (commercial vehicle): Permit violation; fine up to Rs 10,000
- Tampered speed governor: Fine Rs 5,000 (first), Rs 10,000 (repeat); fitness certificate cancelled
