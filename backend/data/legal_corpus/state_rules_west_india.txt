STATE-SPECIFIC RTO RULES — WEST INDIA
Covers: Maharashtra, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Goa, Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu

## MAHARASHTRA

### Maharashtra Motor Vehicles Tax Act, 1958
Road tax rates (one-time / lifetime):
- Two-wheelers up to 99cc: Rs 1,500
- Two-wheelers 100-299cc: Rs 3,000-6,000
- Two-wheelers above 300cc: Rs 8,000-12,000
- Cars up to Rs 10 lakhs: ~10-11% of vehicle cost
- Cars Rs 10-20 lakhs: ~12-13%
- Cars above Rs 20 lakhs: ~14-15%
- Diesel vehicles: Additional 2-3% surcharge
- CNG/LPG vehicles: Reduced rates
- Electric vehicles: Exempted until 2025 (Maharashtra EV Policy)
- Hybrid vehicles: 50% reduction

### Maharashtra-Specific Traffic Rules
(a) Maharashtra follows full central fine rates as per 2019 amendment
(b) Mumbai-specific rules:
    - Heavy vehicle entry: Restricted 7 AM to 11 PM in south Mumbai
    - Auto-rickshaw: Not allowed south of Bandra (west) / Sion (east)
    - Taxi fare: Black-yellow taxis with meter; minimum Rs 25
    - Taxi aggregators: Must comply with MMRTA (Mumbai Metropolitan Region Transport Authority) rules
    - Parking: Pay-and-park system, automated multi-level parking
    - No honking zones: Designated silent zones near hospitals, schools
    - Speed limit: 80 km/h on Eastern/Western Express Highways; 50 km/h in city
(c) Mumbai-Pune Expressway:
    - Toll: FASTag mandatory
    - Speed limit: 100 km/h for cars, 80 km/h for heavy vehicles
    - Two-wheelers above 100cc allowed; below 100cc prohibited
    - No stopping on carriageway
    - Highway patrol active
(d) E-challan: Active across Mumbai, Pune, Solapur, Nashik, Aurangabad
(e) ANPR: Deployed on Mumbai expressways and highways
(f) Vehicle scrapping: Early adoption; RVSFs operational in multiple cities

### Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation (MSRTC)
- "ST" / "Lal Pari" — extensive network
- Online booking: msrtc.gov.in
- Premium: Shivshahi, Ashwamedh, Shivneri (Mumbai-Pune)
- Concessional: Students, senior citizens, disabled persons, freedom fighters

### Pune-Specific Rules
(a) E-challan fully operational
(b) BRTS (Bus Rapid Transit): Dedicated bus lanes
(c) Heavy vehicle restriction: 8 AM to 10 PM in city
(d) Cycle tracks: Dedicated infrastructure in some areas
(e) IT park areas: Special traffic arrangements
(f) Pune-Mumbai Expressway: Strict speed enforcement

## GUJARAT

### Gujarat Motor Vehicles Tax Act, 1958
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 6% for first 7 years (periodic)
- Cars up to 1000cc: 6%
- Cars 1000-1500cc: 7%
- Cars above 1500cc: 7%
- Luxury cars above Rs 20 lakhs: Higher rates
- Electric vehicles: Exempted (Gujarat EV Policy)
- CNG vehicles: Reduced rates (Gujarat promotes CNG heavily)

### Gujarat-Specific Traffic Rules
IMPORTANT: Gujarat significantly reduced central fine rates after 2019 amendment.
Gujarat reduced fines:
- General offence (177): Rs 500 → Rs 250 first, Rs 500 repeat
- Disobedience (179): Rs 2,000 → Rs 1,000
- Driving without licence (181): Rs 5,000 → Rs 3,000
- Speeding LMV (183): Rs 2,000 → Rs 1,000 first, Rs 2,000 repeat
- Dangerous driving (184): Rs 5,000 → Rs 2,500 first, Rs 5,000 repeat
- Drunken driving (185): Rs 10,000 → Rs 5,000 first, Rs 10,000 repeat
- No insurance (194): Rs 2,000 → Rs 1,000 first, Rs 2,000 repeat
- No helmet (194B): Rs 1,000 → Rs 500
- Mobile phone (194D): Rs 5,000 → Rs 1,500 first, Rs 3,000 repeat
- No seat belt (194A): Rs 1,000 → Rs 500

(a) Ahmedabad-specific:
    - BRTS (Janmarg): Dedicated bus lane system
    - Auto-rickshaw: CNG mandatory; meter regulated
    - AMTS (Ahmedabad Municipal Transport Service) buses
    - Speed limit: 60 km/h on SG Highway; 40 km/h in old city
(b) E-challan: Active in Ahmedabad, Surat, Vadodara, Rajkot
(c) Expressway rules: Ahmedabad-Vadodara Expressway — strict enforcement
(d) CNG adoption: Highest in India — most autos and taxis run on CNG
(e) Vibrant Gujarat vehicles: Special permits during events

### Gujarat State Road Transport Corporation (GSRTC)
- Online booking: gsrtc.in
- Premium: Volvo, AC seater services
- Concessional passes for students

### Gujarat RTO
33+ RTOs
Main RTO: Ahmedabad
Website: rtogujarat.gov.in
Online: Gujarat RTO online services portal
Helpline: 079-25501300

## RAJASTHAN

### Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 4-8% of vehicle cost (one-time/periodic depending on district)
- Cars up to 800cc: 5%
- Cars 800-1200cc: 6%
- Cars 1200-2000cc: 7%
- Cars above 2000cc: 8%
- Luxury vehicles above Rs 25 lakhs: 10%
- Electric vehicles: 100% exemption (Rajasthan EV Policy)
- CNG/LPG vehicles: Reduced rates

### Rajasthan-Specific Traffic Rules
IMPORTANT: Rajasthan also reduced some central fine rates.
Rajasthan reduced fines (some key reductions):
- No helmet (194B): Rs 1,000 → Rs 500 first offence
- Mobile phone (194D): Rs 5,000 → Rs 1,000
- No seat belt (194A): Rs 1,000 → Rs 500
Note: Other fines follow central rates or with minor state modifications

(a) Jaipur-specific:
    - Walled city: Heavy vehicle restrictions
    - Tourist area traffic management
    - Auto-rickshaw: Meter + pre-paid at railway station, airport
    - Jaipur Metro integration
    - BRTS: Under development
(b) Highway safety: Strict enforcement on NH 48 (Jaipur-Delhi), NH 8 (Jaipur-Udaipur)
(c) Desert area rules: Fog driving rules in Jodhpur, Bikaner region (winter)
(d) Pilgrimage traffic: Ajmer/Pushkar — special traffic arrangements
(e) Tourist vehicles: State permit required for tourist taxis from other states
(f) E-challan: Active in Jaipur, Jodhpur, Udaipur, Kota, Ajmer

### Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation (RSRTC)
- "Rajasthan Roadways" — extensive network
- Online booking: rsrtc.rajasthan.gov.in
- Premium: Volvo, AC chariot services
- Covers all 33 districts

### Rajasthan RTO
33+ RTOs
Main RTO: Jaipur
Website: transport.rajasthan.gov.in
Online: E-mitra platform for RTO services

## GOA

### Goa Motor Vehicles Tax
Road tax rates (generally lower than mainland states):
- Two-wheelers: 5-8% (one-time)
- Cars up to 1000cc: 7%
- Cars 1000-1500cc: 9%
- Cars above 1500cc: 11%
- Luxury vehicles: 12-15%
- Tourist rental vehicles: Annual tax scheme

### Goa-Specific Traffic Rules
(a) Goa follows central fine rates with some modifications
(b) Tourist-specific rules:
    - Self-drive rental vehicles: Must have Goan permit
    - Two-wheeler rentals: Must have helmet provided; renter must have valid DL
    - Inter-state tourist vehicles: Must register at entry point
    - Beach road restrictions: No heavy vehicles; speed limits
(c) Alcohol rules: Goa has lenient alcohol policy but STRICT drunken driving enforcement
    - BAC limit: Same as national (30 mg/100 ml)
    - Regular checkpoints, especially at night
(d) Speed limits: Generally lower due to narrow roads and tourist areas
(e) Coastal area restrictions: No vehicle access on certain beaches
(f) Mining vehicle rules: Strict weight and route regulations for ore transport
(g) Water sport vehicle rules: Separate licensing for jet skis, boats

### Goa RTO
Kadamba Transport Corporation (KTC) — state bus service
Main RTO: Panaji (North Goa), Margao (South Goa)
Website: goa.gov.in/department/transport
Online services: Limited; being expanded

## DADRA AND NAGAR HAVELI AND DAMAN AND DIU (Union Territory)

### Transport Rules
(a) Combined UT since 2020
(b) Road tax: Generally lower rates (4-8%)
(c) Limited RTO infrastructure
(d) E-challan: Being introduced
(e) Connected to Gujarat highway system
(f) Industrial vehicle traffic: Special permits for factory zones
(g) Border check: Vehicle verification at state borders

### RTO
Single RTO for the UT
Website: dyvsp.daman.nic.in (transport section)
Registration code: DD (Daman & Diu), DN (Dadra & Nagar Haveli)
