COMMERCIAL AND TRANSPORT VEHICLE RULES — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
(Based on Motor Vehicles Act 1988, CMVR 1989, and State Transport Authority Rules)

## PART A — TYPES OF TRANSPORT VEHICLES AND PERMITS

### 1. Goods Carriage
(a) Light Goods Vehicle (LGV): Up to 7,500 kg GVW
(b) Medium Goods Vehicle (MGV): 7,500 to 12,000 kg GVW
(c) Heavy Goods Vehicle (HGV): Above 12,000 kg GVW
(d) Multi-axle vehicle (MAV): Vehicles with 3+ axles
(e) Truck-trailer combination: Tractor with trailer

### 2. Passenger Transport
(a) Stage carriage (bus): Fixed route, fixed fare, schedule service
(b) Contract carriage: Hired for specific trip/duration
    - Tourist vehicles
    - Call taxis / cab services
    - App-based cabs (Ola, Uber, etc.)
(c) Private service vehicle: Company buses, employee transport
(d) Educational institution bus: School buses, college buses
(e) Auto-rickshaws: Three-wheeled passenger vehicles
(f) E-rickshaws: Battery-operated three-wheelers

### 3. Types of Permits
(a) Goods Carriage Permit: For operating goods vehicle within a state
(b) National Permit: For goods vehicles operating across states (validity: all India)
(c) Stage Carriage Permit: For bus operations on fixed routes
(d) Contract Carriage Permit: For taxis, tourist vehicles
(e) Private Service Vehicle Permit: For non-public transport (employee buses)
(f) All India Tourist Permit (AITP): For tourist vehicles operating across states
(g) Special Permit: Temporary permit for specific occasions
(h) Temporary Permit: Valid for 4 months, for specific purpose

## PART B — GOODS VEHICLE RULES

### Loading and Overloading
(a) Maximum permissible weight: As per RC (Gross Vehicle Weight stated in registration)
(b) Overloading penalty (Section 194 read with Section 113):
    - Up to 5% over limit: Warning
    - 5-10% over limit: Fine Rs 2,000 + Rs 1,000 per additional tonne
    - Above 10% over limit: Fine Rs 2,000 + Rs 2,000 per additional tonne; vehicle to be unloaded
(c) Weighbridge: Vehicle can be checked at any government weighbridge
(d) Repeat overloading: Permit may be suspended/cancelled
(e) Axle load limits: As per Central Government notification
(f) Penalty for carrying passengers in goods vehicle: Rs 2,000 base + Rs 1,000 per extra person (Section 182B)

### Documents Required for Goods Vehicle Operation
1. Registration Certificate (RC) — valid
2. Fitness Certificate — valid (annual renewal)
3. Goods Carriage Permit or National Permit — valid
4. Insurance Certificate — valid (TP mandatory + goods-in-transit optional)
5. Driving Licence — valid for the vehicle category (HMV/HGV)
6. PUC Certificate — valid
7. Tax payment receipt (road tax + goods tax where applicable)
8. Way bill / Transport receipt (for goods being carried)
9. E-way bill (for goods value exceeding Rs 50,000 — GST requirement)

### National Permit Rules
(a) Application: To home state transport authority
(b) Validity: 5 years (renewable)
(c) Fee: Annual fee + state authorisation fees (composite fee since 2019)
(d) National Permit number plate: Yellow background with "NP" displayed
(e) Vehicle must comply with fitness standards of all states transited
(f) Composite fee: Single fee covering all state authorisations (replaces per-state fee)
(g) GPS/tracking device: Mandatory for National Permit vehicles

### Hours of Driving
(a) Maximum continuous driving: 4 hours without break
(b) Maximum in 24 hours: 8 hours for goods vehicle, 10 hours for LMV
(c) Mandatory rest: 30 minutes after every 4 hours of driving
(d) Speed governor: Mandatory for all commercial vehicles
    - HGV: Limited to 80 km/h
    - Buses: Limited to 80 km/h (100 km/h on expressways where permitted)
(e) Tachograph / Vehicle tracking: Mandatory for vehicles with National Permit

## PART C — TAXI AND AUTO-RICKSHAW RULES

### Taxi / Cab Rules
(a) Contract Carriage Permit required
(b) Fare meter: Mandatory (electronic digital meter)
(c) Fare chart: Must be displayed inside vehicle
(d) Fare rates: Fixed by state/city transport authority
(e) Refusal to ply: Offence under Section 178 (fine Rs 500)
(f) Overcharging: Offence — complaint to transport authority
(g) Driver must: Display driver ID, keep vehicle clean, follow route requested
(h) AC taxis: Higher fare rate applies (as fixed by authority)
(i) Prepaid taxi: Available at airports, railway stations — fixed fare

### App-Based Cab Aggregator Rules (Section 93, MV Act 2019)
(a) Aggregator must obtain licence from state transport authority
(b) Licence fee: As prescribed by state
(c) Aggregator must:
    - Comply with IT Act and data protection requirements
    - Share ride data with government upon request
    - Display fare estimate before booking
    - Issue electronic receipt after ride
    - Ensure driver and vehicle verification
    - Provide safety features (SOS button, ride sharing, GPS tracking)
    - Maintain insurance for passengers
(d) Surge pricing: Must not exceed prescribed cap (varies by state)
    - Delhi: Maximum 3x base fare
    - Karnataka: Maximum 2x base fare (court-ordered cap)
    - Maharashtra: As per state notification
(e) Penalty for violations: Rs 25,000 (first), up to Rs 1,00,000 (repeat) — Section 193
(f) Licence cancellation for repeated violations

### Auto-Rickshaw Rules
(a) Permit: Contract carriage permit from Regional Transport Authority
(b) Fare meter: Mandatory (electronic, calibrated)
(c) Minimum fare: As fixed by state/city authority (typically Rs 20-30 for first km)
(d) Per km rate: As fixed by authority (typically Rs 10-20 per km)
(e) Waiting charges: As per meter settings
(f) Refusal to ply: Fine Rs 500 (Section 178); can file complaint to traffic police
(g) Overcharging: Fine; complaint to transport authority
(h) Share auto / pooling: Permitted in some cities (separate permit/route required)
(i) Driver ID: Must be displayed inside auto
(j) Route restrictions: May apply in some cities

### Auto-Rickshaw and Taxi Fare Complaint Process
1. Note vehicle registration number and driver details
2. Note meter reading and actual fare demanded
3. File complaint:
   (a) Traffic police app (city-specific)
   (b) State transport department helpline
   (c) Consumer court (for significant overcharging)
   (d) City traffic police website
4. Common complaint channels:
   - Delhi: 1095 (traffic helpline)
   - Mumbai: 103 (traffic helpline)
   - Bangalore: 080-22942222
   - Chennai: 103
   - Kolkata: 1073

## PART D — BUS AND PUBLIC TRANSPORT RULES

### Stage Carriage (Bus) Regulations
(a) Stage carriage permit required for each route
(b) Route, schedule, and stops fixed by Regional Transport Authority
(c) Fare: Fixed by state transport authority
(d) Bus must display: Route number, origin-destination, fare chart
(e) Conductor must: Issue tickets, maintain order, assist passengers
(f) Reserved seats: For women, elderly, disabled persons, pregnant women
(g) Standing passengers: Limited to as per permit (typically 100% of seating capacity)
(h) Overloading: Offence — permit holder and driver liable

### Safety Requirements for Buses
(a) Fire extinguisher: Mandatory (2 units)
(b) First aid kit: Mandatory
(c) Emergency exit: At least 1 (rear/side)
(d) Emergency hammer: For breaking glass in emergency
(e) GPS tracking: Mandatory for new buses
(f) Speed governor: Mandatory (limited to 80 km/h)
(g) CCTV cameras: Mandatory for interstate buses (recommended for all)
(h) Panic button: For women's safety
(i) Emergency contact numbers: Displayed prominently
(j) Destination board: Illuminated at night

### Interstate Bus Travel
(a) Permit must cover origin and destination states
(b) All India Tourist Permit or interstate agreement required
(c) Private bus operators must have valid permit for each route
(d) Passengers: Must carry valid ticket; penalty for ticketless travel Rs 500 (Section 178)
(e) Luggage limits: As per operator policy; excess charge applies
(f) Night travel: Driver must have co-driver for journeys exceeding 8 hours

## PART E — E-RICKSHAW AND ELECTRIC TRANSPORT VEHICLE RULES

### E-Rickshaw Registration
(a) Must be registered with RTO
(b) Green number plate
(c) Battery-operated only (lead-acid or lithium-ion)
(d) Maximum speed: 25 km/h
(e) Seating capacity: 3 passengers + 1 driver (standard)
(f) Must comply with CMVR and ARAI standards

### E-Rickshaw Driver Requirements
(a) Minimum age: 18 years
(b) Driving licence: E-rickshaw category (simplified test)
(c) Training certificate from authorised training centre
(d) No minimum educational qualification required
(e) Aadhaar card mandatory

### E-Rickshaw Permits and Fees
(a) Many cities/states have simplified permit process for e-rickshaws
(b) Reduced permit fees (or exempt) in many states
(c) No road tax in most states
(d) Pollution check: Not required (electric vehicle)
(e) Insurance: Mandatory (third-party)

### Electric Vehicle Transport Incentives
(a) FAME-II subsidy for commercial EVs
(b) Reduced/zero road tax in many states
(c) Reduced/zero registration fee
(d) Special EV zones in some cities (only EVs allowed)
(e) Incentives for fleet operators to electrify

## PART F — OVERLOADING AND WEIGHT RULES

### Permissible Axle Loads
Single axle (2 tyres): 6,000 kg
Single axle (4 tyres): 10,200 kg
Tandem axle (8 tyres): 19,000 kg
Tridem axle (12 tyres): 24,000 kg

### Maximum Gross Vehicle Weight
2-axle rigid vehicle: 18,000 kg (Revised: 18,500 kg for new trucks)
3-axle rigid vehicle: 25,000 kg (Revised: 28,500 kg)
4-axle rigid vehicle: 31,000 kg (Revised: 35,200 kg)
5-axle articulated vehicle: 36,000 kg (Revised: 40,200 kg)
6-axle articulated vehicle: 40,000 kg (Revised: 44,000 kg)

Note: Revised limits notified by MoRTH in 2018 to increase carrying capacity

### Overloading Detection
(a) Static weighbridges at checkpoints
(b) Weigh-in-motion (WIM) systems on highways
(c) Portable weighing equipment
(d) Random checks by RTO enforcement officers

### Penalties for Overloading
(a) Fine: Rs 2,000 + Rs 1,000 per additional tonne (or part thereof)
(b) Vehicle detained until excess load removed
(c) Permit can be suspended for repeated overloading
(d) Road damage caused: Owner liable for repair costs
(e) Criminal liability if overloading causes accident

## PART G — FITNESS CERTIFICATE AND VEHICLE INSPECTION

### Applicability
Mandatory for all transport vehicles (goods and passenger)
Not required for personal/non-transport vehicles until 15 years old

### Fitness Test Components
1. Braking efficiency (service brake and parking brake)
2. Steering alignment and play
3. Suspension condition
4. Tyre condition and tread depth (minimum 1.6 mm)
5. Lighting system (headlights, tail lights, indicators, brake lights)
6. Horn functionality
7. Windshield wiper functionality
8. Rear-view mirrors
9. Exhaust emissions (BS-VI compliance)
10. Speedometer accuracy
11. Body/chassis condition
12. Safety equipment (fire extinguisher, first aid kit, reflectors)
13. Glass (windshield) condition (no cracks obstructing vision)

### Fitness Certificate Validity
New transport vehicle: 2 years from date of registration
Subsequent renewals: 1 year
Application for renewal: 30-60 days before expiry

### Automated Testing Stations (ATS)
As per 2019 amendment, government is establishing automated fitness testing stations:
(a) Computer-controlled testing equipment
(b) No manual intervention in pass/fail decision
(c) Reduces corruption in fitness certification
(d) Video recording of entire test
(e) Result uploaded to Vahan portal automatically
(f) Being rolled out in phases across India

## PART H — SPEED GOVERNOR RULES

### Mandatory for
(a) All transport vehicles (goods and passenger)
(b) School buses
(c) All vehicles with GVW above 3,500 kg

### Speed Limits
Light transport vehicles: 80 km/h
Medium/heavy goods vehicles: 80 km/h
Buses: 80 km/h (100 km/h on expressways where state permits)
School buses: 40 km/h

### Requirements
(a) Speed governor must be tamper-proof
(b) Must be of approved type (ARAI/ICAT certified)
(c) Calibration certificate required
(d) Annual inspection
(e) Tampering with speed governor: Fine Rs 5,000 (first), Rs 10,000 (repeat) + fitness certificate cancellation
(f) Speed governor must be connected to vehicle's fuel injection system or electronic control module

## PART I — HAZARDOUS GOODS TRANSPORT

### What Constitutes Hazardous Goods
As per CMVR and UN classification:
Class 1: Explosives
Class 2: Gases (compressed, liquefied, dissolved)
Class 3: Flammable liquids (petrol, diesel, solvents)
Class 4: Flammable solids
Class 5: Oxidising substances
Class 6: Toxic and infectious substances
Class 7: Radioactive materials
Class 8: Corrosive substances
Class 9: Miscellaneous dangerous goods

### Requirements for Hazardous Goods Transport
(a) Special permit from transport authority
(b) Vehicle must display hazard placards (diamond-shaped with UN number)
(c) Tremcard (Transport Emergency Card) must be carried
(d) Driver must undergo special training
(e) Emergency equipment: Fire extinguisher (2), first aid, spill kit, PPE
(f) Route restrictions: May not pass through populated areas, near schools/hospitals
(g) Time restrictions: May apply in certain cities
(h) Insurance: Special hazardous goods insurance required
(i) Penalty for violation: Rs 3,000 (first), Rs 5,000 (repeat) + imprisonment up to 1 year (Section 191)

### Petroleum/LPG Transport
Additional regulations under:
(a) Petroleum Rules, 2002
(b) Static and Mobile Pressure Vessels Rules
(c) PESO (Petroleum and Explosives Safety Organisation) guidelines
(d) Vehicle must have spark arrestor
(e) "FLAMMABLE" and "NO SMOKING" signs displayed
(f) Grounding chain to prevent static electricity
(g) Emergency valve to stop flow in case of accident
