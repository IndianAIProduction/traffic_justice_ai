DRIVING LICENCE RULES — ALL CATEGORIES AND PROCEDURES
Applicable Across All Indian States and Union Territories
(Based on Motor Vehicles Act 1988, CMVR 1989, and State-specific amendments)

## PART A — TYPES OF DRIVING LICENCES IN INDIA

### 1. Learner's Licence (LL)
Purpose: Allows a person to learn driving on public roads under supervision
Validity: 6 months from date of issue
Conditions:
- Must be accompanied by a licensed driver for the same vehicle class
- Must display "L" sign on vehicle (front and rear)
- Cannot drive between sunset and sunrise
- Cannot carry passengers (except instructor/accompanying licence holder)
- Cannot drive on highways/expressways
Application: Form 1 (combined form after 2019 amendment) — online via Sarathi portal or at RTO
Fee: Rs 150-200 (varies by state)

### 2. Permanent Driving Licence (DL)
Purpose: Authorises holder to drive specified category of motor vehicles
Validity: 20 years from issue date or until age 40 (whichever is later); 10 years for those above 40
Application: After holding LL for minimum 30 days; Form 4 / Form 1 (combined)
Test: Driving test at RTO or authorised testing centre

### 3. International Driving Permit (IDP)
Purpose: Allows driving in foreign countries signatory to Geneva/Vienna conventions
Validity: 1 year from date of issue
Prerequisite: Valid Indian driving licence
Application: At RTO; requires photographs, passport copy, valid DL
Countries: Valid in 150+ countries (not accepted in some countries — check with destination embassy)

### 4. Duplicate Driving Licence
Issued when: Original licence is lost, stolen, or damaged
Application: Form 1 (combined form) at RTO or online
Documents: FIR copy (if lost/stolen), affidavit, photographs
Fee: As prescribed by state (typically Rs 200-500)

### 5. Commercial/Transport Driving Licence
For: Driving transport vehicles (taxis, buses, trucks, goods vehicles)
Additional requirements: LMV licence for 1+ year, medical certificate (Form 1-A), training from accredited school
Validity: 3 years (renewable with medical certificate)
Medical fitness: Must be renewed with each DL renewal

## PART B — VEHICLE CATEGORIES AND LICENCE CLASSES

### MC 50CC (Motorcycle up to 50cc)
Minimum age: 16 years (with parent/guardian consent)
Vehicles covered: Mopeds, electric scooters up to 50cc equivalent
Test: Simplified riding test

### MCWOG (Motorcycle Without Gear)
Minimum age: 18 years
Vehicles covered: Scooters, scooty, automatic transmission two-wheelers above 50cc (e.g., Honda Activa, TVS Jupiter)
Test: Riding test on automatic vehicle

### MCWG (Motorcycle With Gear)
Minimum age: 18 years
Vehicles covered: All motorcycles with manual gear transmission (e.g., Royal Enfield, Bajaj Pulsar, Hero Splendor)
Test: Riding test on manual gear motorcycle
Note: MCWG licence covers MCWOG vehicles also

### LMV-NT (Light Motor Vehicle — Non-Transport)
Minimum age: 18 years
Vehicles covered: Private cars, jeeps, vans up to 7500 kg GVW (e.g., Maruti Swift, Hyundai Creta, Toyota Innova)
Test: Full driving test
Note: Does not authorise driving for hire/commercial purposes

### LMV (Light Motor Vehicle — including Transport)
Minimum age: 18 years (20 for transport use)
Vehicles covered: All vehicles up to 7500 kg GVW, including for hire/transport
Prerequisite for transport: LMV-NT licence for 1+ year
Note: Required for commercial taxi driving, small goods vehicles

### MGV (Medium Goods Vehicle)
Minimum age: 20 years
Vehicles covered: Goods vehicles between 7500 kg and 12000 kg GVW
Prerequisite: LMV licence for 1+ year; medical certificate
Test: Driving test on appropriate vehicle

### HMV (Heavy Motor Vehicle)
Minimum age: 20 years
Vehicles covered: All motor vehicles above 12000 kg GVW
Subcategories:
- HGV (Heavy Goods Vehicle) — trucks, trailers
- HPMV (Heavy Passenger Motor Vehicle) — buses
Prerequisite: LMV licence for 1+ year; medical certificate; formal driver training
Test: Comprehensive driving test

### Adapted Vehicle
For: Persons with physical disabilities
Special features: Vehicle modified with hand controls, wheelchair accessibility, etc.
Test: Modified test appropriate to the disability
Note: Licence specifies the adaptations required

### E-Rickshaw / E-Cart
Minimum age: 18 years
Vehicles covered: Battery-operated e-rickshaws and e-carts
Test: Simplified test
Special: Many states have separate e-rickshaw licence category

### TRAILER
Vehicles covered: Trailer attached to a motor vehicle
Prerequisite: HMV licence
Note: Separate endorsement required on existing HMV licence

## PART C — DRIVING LICENCE APPLICATION PROCESS

### Step 1: Documents Required
For all applicants:
1. Age proof (any one): Birth certificate, Class 10 mark sheet, Passport, Aadhaar card, PAN card
2. Address proof (any one): Aadhaar card, Voter ID, Passport, Utility bill (electricity/gas/water — not older than 3 months), Ration card, Bank passbook/statement
3. Passport-size photographs (6 copies, white background)
4. Blood group certificate (from recognised lab or blood bank)
5. Existing LL (for DL application)

For transport vehicle licence (additional):
6. Medical certificate in Form 1-A from registered medical practitioner
7. Training certificate from accredited driving training school
8. Educational qualification: Minimum Class 8 pass (as per 2019 amendment)
9. Existing LMV DL (held for at least 1 year)

### Step 2: Learner's Licence Application
Online process (via Sarathi portal — sarathi.parivahan.gov.in):
1. Visit Sarathi portal → Select state → Select "New Learner's Licence"
2. Fill application form with personal details
3. Upload documents (scanned copies)
4. Pay fee online (debit card / net banking / UPI)
5. Book slot for LL test at RTO
6. Appear for test (computer-based multiple-choice at most RTOs)
7. Upon passing, LL is issued (sent by post or download from portal)

Offline process:
1. Visit RTO with documents
2. Fill Form 1 (combined application form)
3. Pay fee at RTO counter
4. Appear for LL test
5. LL issued on passing

### Step 3: Driving Licence Application (after 30 days of LL)
Online process:
1. Visit Sarathi portal → Select "New Driving Licence"
2. Enter LL number
3. Fill application form
4. Upload documents
5. Pay fee online
6. Book slot for driving test at RTO
7. Appear for driving test at RTO on scheduled date
8. Upon passing, DL is dispatched (smart card format, sent by registered post)

### Step 4: Driving Test
The test evaluates:
For four-wheelers:
1. Starting the vehicle
2. Driving forward and in reverse
3. Hill start (starting on an incline)
4. Figure-of-eight
5. Parallel parking
6. Stopping at a designated point
7. Traffic rule knowledge
8. Mirror usage
9. Indicator usage

For two-wheelers:
1. Figure-of-eight
2. Balance at slow speed
3. Emergency braking
4. Slalom (weaving through cones)

### Step 5: DL Delivery
- Smart card DL dispatched via registered post within 30 days
- Can track status on Sarathi portal
- Digital DL available on DigiLocker / mParivahan app (legally valid)

## PART D — DRIVING LICENCE RENEWAL

### When to Renew
- Application can be made up to 1 year before expiry (recommended: 30 days before)
- Grace period: Up to 1 year after expiry (with late fee; no re-test required)
- After 1 year of expiry: Re-test may be required at RTO's discretion
- After 5 years of expiry: Full re-test mandatory

### Renewal Process
Online:
1. Sarathi portal → "DL Renewal"
2. Enter DL number and date of birth
3. Fill renewal application
4. Upload latest photograph and documents
5. Pay renewal fee online
6. Book appointment (if medical test required — for transport licence or persons above 40 years)
7. New DL dispatched

### Renewal Fee
- Non-transport: Rs 200-500 (varies by state)
- Transport: Rs 200-500 + medical certificate fee
- Late fee: Rs 200-1,000 (depending on delay and state)

### Medical Certificate for Renewal
Required for:
- Transport vehicle DL (every renewal)
- Persons above 40 years (every renewal)
- Persons with known medical conditions
Medical test: Eye test, general physical fitness, diabetes check, blood pressure

## PART E — CHANGE OF ADDRESS ON DRIVING LICENCE

### When Required
When licence holder changes residential address (within same state or to another state)

### Process
Online: Sarathi portal → "Change of Address"
Documents: New address proof, existing DL
Fee: Rs 200-300 (varies by state)
Note: If moving to another state, some states require fresh DL; most allow address update

## PART F — ENDORSEMENT AND SUSPENSION OF LICENCE

### Endorsement
When a traffic violation is committed, the convicting magistrate or compounding officer may endorse the violation on the driving licence.
Three endorsements within 3 years: Licence may be suspended.

### Suspension of Licence
Grounds:
1. Three or more endorsements within 3 years
2. Conviction for dangerous driving (Section 184)
3. Conviction for drunken driving (Section 185)
4. Conviction for racing (Section 189)
5. Not wearing helmet (Section 194B — 3-month suspension)
6. Overloading two-wheeler (Section 194F — 3-month suspension)

Duration: As ordered by magistrate or licensing authority (minimum 3 months, maximum 2 years for most offences)

### Revocation of Licence
Grounds:
1. Licence obtained by fraud/misrepresentation
2. Holder is physically/mentally unfit to drive
3. Repeated serious offences
4. Court order

### Appeal
- Against licensing authority's order: Appeal to State Transport Appellate Tribunal
- Against court order: Appeal to Sessions Court / High Court
- Time limit for appeal: 30 days from date of order

## PART G — DIGITAL DRIVING LICENCE AND MODERN FEATURES

### DigiLocker
- Digital DL available on DigiLocker (digilocker.gov.in)
- Legally valid as per IT Act, 2000
- Can be shown to police officers during verification

### mParivahan App
- Mobile app by Ministry of Road Transport
- Shows virtual DL and RC
- Accepts digital documents during verification
- Available on Android and iOS
- Features: DL details, RC details, challan status, PUC status, FASTag details

### Aadhaar-linked DL
- DL linked to Aadhaar for identity verification
- Enables paperless verification
- Reduces fraud in DL issuance

## PART I — COMMON DL-RELATED PROBLEMS AND SOLUTIONS

### Problem: Lost driving licence
Solution:
1. File FIR at nearest police station
2. Apply for duplicate DL online or at RTO
3. Carry affidavit stating loss of DL
4. Use DigiLocker/mParivahan as interim proof

### Problem: Expired driving licence
Solution:
1. If expired less than 1 year: Renew online, no re-test
2. If expired 1-5 years: May need re-test, apply at RTO
3. If expired more than 5 years: Full re-test required
4. During renewal processing, carry renewal acknowledgement receipt

### Problem: Address change not updated
Solution:
1. Update online via Sarathi portal
2. Fine for not updating within 30 days: Not enforced strictly but may cause issues during verification

### Problem: DL shows wrong details
Solution:
1. Apply for correction at RTO
2. Submit supporting documents
3. No fee for correction of RTO's error

### Problem: DL suspended/endorsed
Solution:
1. Wait for suspension period to end
2. Apply for restoration at RTO
3. May need to complete driving improvement course (in some states)
4. Can appeal suspension order within 30 days

### Problem: Challaned for wrong vehicle category
Solution:
1. If licence doesn't cover the vehicle class, you are liable
2. Get appropriate endorsement added to licence
3. To add vehicle class: Apply at RTO, pass test for new category
