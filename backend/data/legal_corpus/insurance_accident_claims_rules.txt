MOTOR VEHICLE INSURANCE AND ACCIDENT CLAIMS — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
(Based on Motor Vehicles Act 1988 (Chapter XI & XII), IRDAI Regulations, and Supreme Court Guidelines)

## PART A — TYPES OF MOTOR VEHICLE INSURANCE

### 1. Third-Party Liability Insurance (Mandatory)
Coverage:
- Death or bodily injury to any third party (not the insured driver)
- Damage to third-party property (limit: Rs 7.5 lakhs per incident as per IRDAI)
- Legal liability of the insured vehicle owner
Not covered:
- Damage to own vehicle
- Personal injury to the driver/owner
- Theft of own vehicle
Premium: Fixed by IRDAI annually (not negotiable)
Validity: 1 year (must be renewed annually; 3-year TP cover for new cars available; 5-year for two-wheelers)

### 2. Comprehensive Insurance (Optional but Recommended)
Coverage:
- All of third-party coverage
- Damage to own vehicle due to: Accident, fire, theft, natural calamity, riot, terrorism
- Personal accident cover for owner-driver (Rs 15 lakhs mandatory)
Additional covers (add-on):
- Zero depreciation
- Engine protection
- Road side assistance
- Return to invoice
- NCB (No Claim Bonus) protection
- Key and lock replacement
- Consumables cover
- Tyre protection
- Passenger personal accident cover
Premium: TP portion fixed by IRDAI + OD (own damage) portion varies by insurer
Validity: 1 year

### 3. Standalone Own Damage (OD) Policy
Available for: Vehicle owners who have separate TP policy
Coverage: Only own damage to vehicle
Premium: Varies by insurer, vehicle age, IDV (Insured Declared Value)

### 4. Pay-As-You-Drive (PAYG) Insurance
New option: Premium linked to actual distance driven
Available from: Select insurers
Benefits: Lower premium for low-usage vehicles

## PART B — KEY INSURANCE CONCEPTS

### Insured Declared Value (IDV)
- Maximum amount payable in case of total loss / theft
- Calculated as: Manufacturer's listed selling price minus depreciation
- Depreciation schedule:
  Vehicle age | Depreciation
  Up to 6 months | 5%
  6 months to 1 year | 15%
  1-2 years | 20%
  2-3 years | 30%
  3-4 years | 40%
  4-5 years | 50%
  Above 5 years | As per agreement between insurer and insured

### No Claim Bonus (NCB)
- Discount on OD premium for each claim-free year
- NCB schedule:
  After 1 claim-free year | 20%
  After 2 claim-free years | 25%
  After 3 claim-free years | 35%
  After 4 claim-free years | 45%
  After 5+ claim-free years | 50%
- NCB belongs to the owner, not the vehicle
- NCB can be transferred to new vehicle
- NCB lost if claim is made during the year

### Deductible / Excess
- Compulsory deductible: Rs 1,000 (two-wheeler), Rs 2,000 (four-wheeler) — amount you pay from own pocket per claim
- Voluntary deductible: Additional amount you agree to pay (Rs 2,500 to Rs 15,000) in exchange for lower premium

## PART C — FILING AN INSURANCE CLAIM

### Claim Process for Own Damage
1. Intimate the insurance company immediately:
   - Through app, website, toll-free number, or email
   - Within 24 hours of incident (preferred; delay may cause scrutiny but cannot be sole ground for rejection)
2. File FIR if: theft, third-party involvement, hit-and-run, or major accident
3. Get survey done: Insurance company appoints a surveyor to assess damage
4. Get vehicle repaired: At network garage (cashless) or any garage (reimbursement)
5. Submit documents: Claim form, RC copy, DL copy, FIR (if applicable), repair bills, photographs
6. Claim settlement: Within 30 days of receiving all documents (as per IRDAI guidelines)

### Claim Process for Third-Party Liability
1. Third party (victim) or their representative files claim before MACT
2. Insurance company is made a party to the proceedings
3. MACT awards compensation
4. Insurance company pays as per tribunal order
5. Insurance company may recover from insured if policy conditions were violated (e.g., unlicensed driver)

### Documents Required for Claim
For accident:
1. Claim form (insurance company's format)
2. RC copy
3. DL copy of driver at time of accident
4. FIR copy / police complaint
5. Photographs of damaged vehicle
6. Repair estimate and bills
7. Medical bills (for personal accident claims)
8. Policy copy

For theft:
1. FIR copy
2. Final police report (non-traceable report) — typically after 3-6 months of investigation
3. RC original
4. All keys of the vehicle
5. Claim form
6. Policy copy
7. NOC from financier (if hypothecated)

## PART D — MOTOR ACCIDENT CLAIMS TRIBUNAL (MACT)

### What is MACT
- Established under Section 165 of MV Act
- Special tribunal for deciding motor accident compensation claims
- Presided by a District Judge-level officer
- No court fee for filing
- Relatively faster than civil courts

### Who Can File
1. Injured person
2. Legal representative of deceased person
3. Agent or attorney of claimant
4. Owner of property damaged in accident (for property damage claims)

### Jurisdiction
MACT has jurisdiction where:
(a) The accident occurred, OR
(b) The claimant resides, OR
(c) The defendant (vehicle owner/driver/insurer) resides
Choice of jurisdiction lies with the claimant.

### Procedure
1. File application before MACT (Form prescribed by High Court)
2. MACT issues notice to:
   - Vehicle owner
   - Vehicle driver
   - Insurance company
3. Respondents file written statement / defence
4. Evidence is recorded:
   - Medical reports
   - Income proof
   - Eyewitness testimony
   - Police investigation report (MVI report)
   - Photographs, CCTV footage
5. Arguments by both sides
6. MACT passes award with compensation amount
7. Appeal: To High Court within 90 days of MACT order

### Compensation Calculation — Supreme Court Guidelines

#### For Death Cases
Based on Sarla Verma v. Delhi Transport Corporation (2009) and National Insurance Co. v. Pranay Sethi (2017):

Multiplier based on age of deceased:
Age of deceased | Multiplier
15-20 years | 18
21-25 years | 17
26-30 years | 16
31-35 years | 15
36-40 years | 14
41-45 years | 13
46-50 years | 12
51-55 years | 11
56-60 years | 10
61-65 years | 8
66-70+ years | 5

Compensation formula:
Annual income × Multiplier = Loss of dependency
Plus fixed amounts (as per Pranay Sethi):
- Funeral expenses: Rs 15,000
- Loss of consortium: Rs 40,000
- Loss of estate: Rs 15,000
- Filial consortium (loss of love of parent for minor child): Rs 40,000

Income considerations:
- Actual income at time of death (with documentary proof)
- 40% increase for future prospects (if deceased was below 40 years, salaried)
- 25% increase for future prospects (if deceased was 40-50 years, salaried)
- For self-employed: Future prospects allowed if income is established
- Deduction for personal expenses of deceased: 1/3 (if spouse + children), 1/4 (if spouse + children + dependent parents), 1/2 (if only spouse)

#### For Injury Cases
Compensation includes:
1. Medical expenses (past and future)
2. Loss of income during treatment/recovery
3. Loss of earning capacity (percentage disability × income × multiplier)
4. Pain and suffering (lump sum — Rs 50,000 to Rs 10 lakhs depending on severity)
5. Attendant charges
6. Transport charges for treatment
7. Special diet/nutrition
8. Prosthetics, aids, and appliances
9. Future medical expenses
10. Loss of amenities of life

### Structured Settlement
- MACT may order payment in instalments
- Interest on compensation: Typically 7.5-9% per annum from date of filing
- Payment deposit by insurer within 30 days of order

## PART E — HIT AND RUN COMPENSATION

### Under Section 161, MV Act (2019 Amendment)
(1) Death: Rs 2,00,000
(2) Grievous hurt: Rs 50,000
(Previously: Rs 25,000 for death and Rs 12,500 for grievous hurt — enhanced by 2019 amendment)

### Who Pays
- Central Government through Solatium Fund
- Fund maintained from contributions by insurance companies

### Process
1. File FIR at nearest police station
2. Apply to Claims Enquiry Officer (CEO) appointed by District Collector
3. Submit: Application form, FIR copy, medical report, post-mortem report (for death), hospital records
4. CEO enquires into the claim
5. CEO submits report to Claims Settlement Commissioner
6. Commissioner awards compensation
7. Payment within 30 days of order

### Time Limit
- Application within 6 months of accident
- Extendable by CEO for "sufficient cause" (courts have been liberal)

### Additional MACT Claim
- Hit-and-run compensation under Section 161 is in ADDITION to any MACT claim
- If offending vehicle is later identified, full MACT compensation can be claimed
- Section 161 amount is adjusted against MACT award

## PART F — INSURANCE DISPUTES AND REMEDIES

### 1. Insurance Ombudsman
- Jurisdiction: Claims up to Rs 30 lakhs
- Free of cost service
- 17 Insurance Ombudsman offices across India
- Complaint within 1 year of insurer's final decision
- Ombudsman decides within 3 months
- Decision binding on insurer (up to Rs 30 lakhs)
- Complainant can accept or reject (if rejects, can approach court)

Ombudsman offices: Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Ahmedabad, Chandigarh, Jaipur, Lucknow, Pune, Bhopal, Bhubaneswar, Guwahati, Kochi, Noida, Patna

### 2. IRDAI Complaint
- File through IGMS portal (igms.irda.gov.in)
- IRDAI ensures insurer responds within 15 days
- Escalation option if insurer doesn't respond

### 3. Consumer Commission
- District Commission: Claims up to Rs 1 crore
- State Commission: Rs 1 crore to Rs 10 crores
- National Commission: Above Rs 10 crores
- Time limit: 2 years from cause of action
- No lawyer required (can argue personally)
- Nominal court fee

### 4. Civil Court
- For claims not covered by other forums
- Higher court fees and longer procedure
- Generally not preferred for insurance disputes

## PART G — IMPORTANT SUPREME COURT JUDGMENTS ON MOTOR INSURANCE

### 1. National Insurance Co. v. Swaran Singh (2004)
Held: If owner's vehicle is driven by an unlicensed driver and accident occurs:
- Insurer must pay third-party claim first
- Insurer can recover from the insured owner later
- Third party's right to compensation cannot be denied due to policy breach by insured

### 2. Sarla Verma v. Delhi Transport Corporation (2009)
Held: Standardised multiplier table for compensation calculation based on age of deceased
Established structured formula for death compensation cases

### 3. National Insurance Co. v. Pranay Sethi (2017)
Held: Fixed amounts for conventional heads of compensation:
- Funeral expenses: Rs 15,000
- Consortium: Rs 40,000
- Estate: Rs 15,000
- Enhanced future prospects calculation

### 4. Bajaj Allianz v. Rambha Devi (2020)
Held: Insurance company cannot deny cashless treatment during Golden Hour
Medical expenses during first hour after accident must be covered

### 5. Oriental Insurance v. Prithvi Raj (2020)
Held: Minor breach of policy condition (like driving without glasses when prescribed) does not void the policy entirely for third-party claims

### 6. New India Assurance v. Sadanand Mukhi (2009)
Held: Non-intimation of accident within specified period is not absolute ground for claim rejection
Delay must be considered with its cause

## PART H — GOLDEN HOUR TREATMENT

### Section 162, MV Act (amended 2019)
1. During golden hour (first hour after accident), cashless treatment at ANY hospital (government or private)
2. Hospital cannot refuse treatment
3. Hospital cannot demand advance payment
4. Treatment cost reimbursed from Motor Vehicle Accident Fund
5. Good Samaritan (anyone who helps) is protected from civil/criminal liability
6. Good Samaritan cannot be compelled to be witness
7. Police cannot detain or question Good Samaritan at hospital

### Motor Vehicle Accident Fund (Section 164B)
- Maintained by Central Government
- Funded from: Road cess, fine collection, penalties, insurance premium surcharge
- Used for: Golden hour treatment, hit-and-run compensation, compensation for no-insurance accidents
- Administered by General Insurance Corporation of India (GIC Re) as trustee

## PART J — FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Can I drive with expired insurance?
A: No. Driving without valid insurance is an offence under Section 196. Fine: Rs 2,000 (first offence), Rs 4,000 (repeat) + possible imprisonment up to 3 months. Vehicle can be impounded.

Q: Can insurance company reject claim if my licence was expired by a few days?
A: Courts have held that minor/technical breach of licence validity (few days/weeks expiry) does not void the policy, especially if driver was otherwise competent. However, this is decided case by case. Best to keep licence valid.

Q: What if accident happens while someone else is driving my vehicle?
A: If the driver had a valid licence, the insurance covers. If driver had no valid licence, insurer may reject own damage claim but must pay third-party claims (and recover from you).

Q: Is third-party insurance mandatory for e-scooters/e-bikes?
A: Yes, for all motor vehicles used in public places, including electric vehicles. Even low-speed EVs (up to 25 km/h) need insurance.

Q: Can I get insurance for a vehicle without registration?
A: New vehicles get temporary insurance before registration. But to continue, the vehicle must be registered. Insurance for unregistered vehicles (other than during transit) is not available.

Q: What happens if the other driver's insurance has lapsed?
A: You can still claim from the other driver's insurer (if policy was active at accident time). If no insurance at all, claim through MACT against the driver/owner personally, and from Motor Vehicle Accident Fund.
