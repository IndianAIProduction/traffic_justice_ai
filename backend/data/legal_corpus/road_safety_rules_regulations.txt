ROAD SAFETY RULES AND REGULATIONS — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
(Based on Motor Vehicles Act 1988, CMVR 1989, Indian Road Congress guidelines, and State Traffic Rules)

## PART A — RULES OF THE ROAD (LEFT-HAND TRAFFIC SYSTEM)

### 1. Fundamental Rules
India follows left-hand traffic (LHT) system. All vehicles must:
(a) Drive on the left side of the road at all times
(b) Overtake only from the right side
(c) At uncontrolled intersections, give way to traffic coming from the right
(d) At roundabouts, give way to traffic already in the roundabout (traffic from the right)
(e) Pedestrians have right of way at zebra crossings
(f) Emergency vehicles have absolute right of way at all times

### 2. Lane Discipline
(a) Slow-moving vehicles: Extreme left lane
(b) Medium-speed vehicles: Middle lane
(c) Fast-moving vehicles: Right lane (for overtaking only on highways)
(d) Two-wheelers: Left lane in city traffic
(e) Heavy vehicles: Extreme left lane on highways
(f) Do not straddle lane markings
(g) Change lanes only after checking mirrors and using indicators
(h) Minimum distance between vehicles: 2-second rule (maintain gap of 2 seconds behind the vehicle ahead)

### 3. Overtaking Rules
(a) Overtake only from the right side
(b) Never overtake on a curve, hill crest, narrow bridge, or intersection
(c) Never overtake when road visibility is less than 150 metres
(d) Use horn or flash headlights to signal intention to overtake
(e) Do not overtake when the vehicle ahead is signalling to turn right
(f) After overtaking, return to the left lane safely
(g) The overtaken vehicle must not increase speed
(h) On single-lane roads with two-way traffic, overtake only when the opposite lane is clear
(i) Never overtake on a continuous white/yellow centre line

### 4. Right of Way
Priority order at intersections:
(a) Emergency vehicles (ambulance, fire brigade, police)
(b) Vehicles on the main road (over vehicles from side roads)
(c) Vehicles from the right (at uncontrolled intersections)
(d) Vehicles already in the roundabout
(e) Pedestrians at zebra crossings

### 5. Turning Rules
Left turn:
(a) Stay in the left lane
(b) Signal left well in advance (at least 30 metres before turn)
(c) Slow down and check for pedestrians and cyclists
(d) Give way to pedestrians crossing the road you are turning into

Right turn:
(a) Move to the centre of the road or right-turn lane
(b) Signal right well in advance
(c) Give way to oncoming traffic
(d) Turn only when safe, without obstructing other traffic

U-turn:
(a) Permitted only where allowed (no U-turn signs must be obeyed)
(b) Signal and check mirrors before U-turn
(c) Give way to all other traffic
(d) Never make U-turn on highways/expressways (use designated turnarounds)

### 6. One-Way Roads
(a) Follow the direction indicated by signs
(b) When entering a one-way road, give way to existing traffic
(c) On one-way roads, overtaking can be from either side (but left overtaking is not recommended)
(d) Penalty for driving against one-way: Rs 500 to Rs 1,500 (Section 177)

## PART B — TRAFFIC SIGNALS AND SIGNS

### 1. Traffic Light Signals
RED: Stop. Do not cross the stop line. Vehicles must wait behind the stop line.
FREE LEFT TURN: Left turn allowed on red signal where indicated (varies by city)
YELLOW/AMBER: Prepare to stop. Do not enter intersection if safely able to stop.
GREEN: Proceed with caution. Give way to pedestrians already crossing.
GREEN ARROW: Proceed only in direction of arrow.
FLASHING RED: Treat as stop sign. Stop completely and proceed when safe.
FLASHING YELLOW: Proceed with caution. Slow down.

### 2. Mandatory Signs (Circular with Red Border)
STOP: Complete stop required
GIVE WAY: Slow down and give way to traffic on main road
NO ENTRY: Entry prohibited from this direction
ONE WAY: Traffic flows in indicated direction only
SPEED LIMIT: Maximum speed allowed (number inside circle)
NO PARKING: Parking prohibited
NO STOPPING: No stopping or standing
NO HORN: Horn use prohibited
NO OVERTAKING: Overtaking prohibited
NO U-TURN: U-turn prohibited
NO LEFT TURN / NO RIGHT TURN: Turn in indicated direction prohibited
WEIGHT LIMIT: Vehicles above indicated weight prohibited
HEIGHT LIMIT: Vehicles above indicated height prohibited
COMPULSORY TURN: Must turn in indicated direction
COMPULSORY KEEP LEFT: Must stay on left side of divider/island

### 3. Cautionary/Warning Signs (Triangular with Red Border)
RIGHT CURVE / LEFT CURVE: Road curves ahead
SHARP BEND: Sharp turn ahead
STEEP ASCENT / STEEP DESCENT: Hill ahead
NARROW ROAD: Road narrows ahead
NARROW BRIDGE: Bridge is narrower than road
SCHOOL AHEAD: School zone — slow down
PEDESTRIAN CROSSING: Zebra crossing ahead
SPEED BREAKER: Speed bump/breaker ahead
RAILWAY CROSSING (WITH/WITHOUT GATE): Level crossing ahead
FALLING ROCKS: Rockfall zone
SLIPPERY ROAD: Road may be slippery when wet
MEN AT WORK: Construction zone
CYCLE CROSSING: Bicycle crossing ahead
CATTLE: Animals may cross road
GAP IN MEDIAN: Opening in road divider ahead

### 4. Informatory Signs (Rectangular, Blue/Green)
HOSPITAL: Hospital nearby
PETROL PUMP: Fuel station nearby
PARKING: Parking area available
FIRST AID: First aid post nearby
TELEPHONE: Public telephone nearby
EATING PLACE: Restaurant nearby
REST AREA: Rest stop nearby
NO THROUGH ROAD: Dead end
TOURIST PLACE: Point of interest ahead
HIGHWAY DISTANCE MARKERS: Distance to next city/town

### 5. Road Markings
WHITE CONTINUOUS LINE (centre): No overtaking, no crossing
WHITE BROKEN LINE (centre): Overtaking permitted if safe
YELLOW SINGLE LINE (edge): No parking on this side
YELLOW DOUBLE LINE (edge): No parking and no stopping
ZEBRA CROSSING: Black and white stripes — pedestrian crossing; vehicles must stop if pedestrians are crossing
STOP LINE: Thick white line at intersections — vehicles must stop behind this line
BOX JUNCTION: Yellow hatched box — do not enter unless exit is clear
SPEED LIMIT MARKINGS: Speed painted on road surface
BUS LANE MARKINGS: Reserved lane for buses
CYCLE LANE MARKINGS: Reserved lane for bicycles

## PART C — SPEED LIMITS IN INDIA

### National Speed Limits (Default)
Vehicle Type | City | State Highway | National Highway | Expressway
Passenger cars | 50 km/h | 80 km/h | 100 km/h | 120 km/h
Two-wheelers | 40 km/h | 60 km/h | 80 km/h | Not allowed*
Auto-rickshaws | 35 km/h | 50 km/h | Not allowed | Not allowed
Light goods vehicle | 50 km/h | 70 km/h | 80 km/h | 100 km/h
Heavy goods vehicle | 40 km/h | 60 km/h | 60-80 km/h | 80 km/h
Buses | 40 km/h | 70 km/h | 80 km/h | 100 km/h
School buses | 40 km/h | 40 km/h | 40 km/h | Not allowed
Tractors | 25 km/h | 30 km/h | Not allowed | Not allowed

*Two-wheelers may be restricted on certain expressways (e.g., Mumbai-Pune Expressway allows two-wheelers above 100cc)

### Special Zone Speed Limits
School zones: 25 km/h
Hospital zones: 25 km/h
Residential areas: 30 km/h
Construction zones: As marked (typically 20-30 km/h)

### State-Specific Speed Variations
States may set different speed limits from the national default:
- Delhi: City limit 50 km/h for cars; 40 km/h for commercial vehicles
- Maharashtra: 80 km/h on Mumbai-Pune Expressway for heavy vehicles
- Karnataka: 100 km/h on state highways for cars
- Tamil Nadu: 90 km/h on national highways for cars
Note: Always follow posted speed limit signs which override default limits.

## PART D — PARKING RULES

### Legal Parking
(a) Park only in designated parking areas or where parking is permitted
(b) Park parallel to the road edge, close to the left kerb
(c) In marked parking zones, park within marked bay only
(d) Pay parking fee where applicable (many cities have automated parking systems)
(e) Display parking receipt/token on dashboard where required

### Illegal Parking (No Parking Zones)
You must NOT park:
(a) At or near road intersections (within 50 metres)
(b) On or near pedestrian crossings
(c) On footpaths or cycle tracks
(d) Near fire hydrants
(e) At bus stops
(f) On bridges or flyovers
(g) Near traffic signals
(h) In front of entrances to buildings/driveways
(i) On the main carriageway of national highways
(j) Where no-parking signs are displayed
(k) On double yellow line marked roads (no stopping)
(l) On single yellow line marked roads during restricted hours

### Parking Penalties
Wrong parking (Section 177): Rs 500 (first offence), Rs 1,500 (repeat)
City-specific towing charges:
- Delhi: Rs 400 (two-wheeler), Rs 700-2,000 (four-wheeler) + Rs 150-500/day storage
- Mumbai: Rs 500-2,500 + storage charges
- Bangalore: Rs 500-2,000 + storage charges
- Chennai: Rs 400-1,500 + storage charges
Note: Towing charges vary by city and are set by municipal/traffic authorities

## PART E — HELMET AND SEATBELT RULES

### Helmet Rules (Section 129 read with Section 194B)
(1) Helmet is mandatory for:
    (a) Rider (driver) of any two-wheeler
    (b) Pillion rider (passenger on rear)
    (c) Child pillion rider (child helmet required)
(2) Helmet must conform to BIS standard IS 4151
    (a) ISI mark must be present on helmet
    (b) Must have chin strap fastened while riding
    (c) Non-ISI helmets provide no legal compliance
    (d) Visor must not be excessively tinted (50%+ light transmission required)
(3) Exemption: Sikh persons wearing turban are exempt (Section 129)
(4) Penalty: Rs 1,000 + 3-month licence disqualification (Section 194B)
(5) Non-standard helmets (cricket helmet, construction helmet): Not valid

### Seatbelt Rules (Section 194A, 194E)
(1) Seatbelt is mandatory for:
    (a) Driver of any four-wheeler vehicle
    (b) Front seat passenger
    (c) Rear seat passengers (mandatory since 2022 amendment enforcement)
(2) Penalty for not wearing seatbelt:
    (a) Driver: Rs 1,000 (Section 194A)
    (b) Passengers: Rs 1,000 (Section 194E)
(3) Exception: Vehicle manufactured without seatbelts (very old vehicles)
(4) Child safety: Children under 12 years should sit in rear with seatbelt
    - Child car seats recommended for children under 4 years

## PART F — DRUNKEN DRIVING AND DRUG DRIVING

### Legal Limits
Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC): Maximum 30 mg of alcohol per 100 ml of blood
This is effectively near-zero tolerance (approximately 1 small drink may exceed this)
For commercial/transport drivers: Zero tolerance in many states

### Detection
(a) Breathalyser (primary method): Police can stop any vehicle and conduct breathalyser test
(b) Blood test: If breathalyser shows positive, blood sample collected
(c) Clinical examination: By registered medical practitioner
(d) Refusal to undergo test: Presumption of intoxication under Section 203

### Penalties (Section 185)
First offence: Fine up to Rs 10,000 AND/OR imprisonment up to 6 months
Repeat offence (within 3 years): Fine up to Rs 15,000 AND/OR imprisonment up to 2 years
Additional consequences:
- Licence suspension/cancellation
- Vehicle impoundment
- If accident occurs under influence: Enhanced penalty under Section 184 (dangerous driving)
- If death occurs under influence: Section 304A IPC (negligent homicide) + Section 185 MV Act

### Drug Driving
Same penalties as drunken driving under Section 185
Includes: Cannabis, cocaine, opioids, amphetamines, benzodiazepines, and any intoxicating drug
Detection: Urine test, blood test, clinical examination

### State-Specific Enforcement
- Delhi: Aggressive enforcement, especially during festivals (zero tolerance drives)
- Mumbai: Regular checkpoints, breathalyser van deployment
- Bangalore: Night-time checkpoints, especially on pub/restaurant zones
- Chennai: Regular enforcement, court appearance required for repeat offenders
- Hyderabad: Drive-through checkpoints, awareness campaigns
Most states conduct special drives during: New Year, Diwali, Holi, weekends

## PART G — SCHOOL ZONE AND CHILD SAFETY

### School Bus Rules
(a) School bus must be painted yellow
(b) "SCHOOL BUS" written prominently on front and rear
(c) Speed limit: 40 km/h at all times
(d) Must have speed governor
(e) First aid kit mandatory
(f) Fire extinguisher mandatory
(g) Driver must have: Valid HMV/HPMV licence, police verification, minimum 5 years driving experience
(h) Female attendant mandatory on each school bus
(i) GPS tracking mandatory
(j) Emergency exit clearly marked
(k) CCTV cameras recommended

### Juvenile Driving (Section 199)
(a) Minors (below 18 years) driving any motor vehicle is a serious offence
(b) Parent/guardian of the minor: Fine Rs 25,000 + imprisonment up to 3 years
(c) Owner of the vehicle: Same liability as guardian
(d) Vehicle registration: Cancelled
(e) Minor: Tried under Juvenile Justice Act
(f) Insurance: Void — no coverage for accidents caused by minor drivers

## PART H — HIGHWAY AND EXPRESSWAY RULES

### National Highway Rules
(a) Minimum speed: As posted (typically 60-80 km/h for cars)
(b) Maximum speed: As posted (typically 100 km/h for cars)
(c) No stopping on carriageway (use hard shoulder only for emergencies)
(d) No reversing on highway
(e) No U-turn except at designated locations
(f) Use hazard lights when stopping on shoulder
(g) Reflective triangles to be placed behind vehicle if breakdown (at least 50 metres behind)
(h) No pedestrian movement on main carriageway
(i) No crossing highway on foot (use foot over bridge/underpass)
(j) Toll payment: Cash, FASTag (mandatory), or UPI

### Expressway Rules (Additional)
(a) FASTag mandatory for entry (no cash lane)
(b) Minimum speed: 80 km/h (varies)
(c) Maximum speed: 120 km/h (varies)
(d) Certain vehicle types restricted: Tractors, animal-drawn vehicles, auto-rickshaws
(e) Two-wheelers may be restricted or allowed based on engine capacity
(f) Breakdown: Move vehicle to hard shoulder, use hazard lights, call highway helpline
(g) Wrong-side driving on expressway: Severe penalty + licence suspension

### FASTag
(a) Mandatory for all four-wheelers using toll roads (since February 2021)
(b) Electronic toll collection using RFID tag
(c) Available from: Banks, Paytm, Amazon, dedicated offices at toll plazas
(d) Linked to: Prepaid wallet or bank account
(e) Penalty for not having FASTag: Double toll charged at toll plaza
(f) Annual tag fee: Rs 100-200
(g) Minimum balance requirement: Rs 100

## PART I — EMERGENCY VEHICLE RULES

### Right of Way (Section 194C)
(1) ALL vehicles must give way to emergency vehicles:
    (a) Ambulances
    (b) Fire brigade vehicles
    (c) Police vehicles on emergency duty
    (d) Disaster management vehicles
(2) Penalty for not giving way: Rs 10,000 (Section 194C)
(3) How to give way:
    (a) Slow down and move to the left
    (b) If at intersection, do not block the path of emergency vehicle
    (c) If on single-lane road, stop and allow emergency vehicle to pass

### Blue Beacon/Siren
Only authorised vehicles can use:
(a) Red beacon: Emergency vehicles (ambulance, fire brigade)
(b) Blue beacon: Police, military, authorised government vehicles
(c) Use of unauthorized beacon/siren is an offence — fine Rs 5,000

## PART J — ROAD ACCIDENT STATISTICS AND AWARENESS

### Key Statistics (Ministry of Road Transport and Highways)
(a) India accounts for approximately 11% of global road accident deaths
(b) Major causes of road accidents:
    - Overspeeding: ~70% of accidents
    - Drunken driving: ~5% of fatal accidents
    - Wrong-side driving: ~8% of accidents
    - Distracted driving (mobile phone): ~3% of accidents
    - Others (poor road condition, vehicle defects, weather): ~14%
(c) Most vulnerable road users: Two-wheeler riders (37%), pedestrians (15%), cyclists
(d) Peak accident hours: 6 PM to 9 PM (visibility transition + rush hour)

### Road Safety Tips
1. Always wear seatbelt (reduces fatality risk by 45-50%)
2. Always wear helmet (reduces head injury risk by 70%)
3. Never drink and drive
4. Follow speed limits
5. Maintain safe following distance (2-second rule; 4-second in rain)
6. Do not use mobile phone while driving
7. Check mirrors before changing lanes
8. Use indicators/turn signals
9. Be extra careful at intersections
10. Be visible (use lights in low visibility conditions)
11. Slow down in rain, fog, and poor visibility
12. Take regular breaks on long drives (every 2-3 hours)
13. Avoid driving when fatigued or drowsy
14. Ensure vehicle is in good condition (brakes, tyres, lights)
15. Keep first aid kit in vehicle
