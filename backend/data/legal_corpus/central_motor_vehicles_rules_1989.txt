CENTRAL MOTOR VEHICLES RULES, 1989
(As amended up to 2024)
Ministry of Road Transport and Highways, Government of India

## CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY

Rule 1. Short title and commencement
These rules may be called the Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989.

Rule 2. Definitions
In these rules, unless the context otherwise requires:
(a) "Act" means the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 (59 of 1988);
(b) "Form" means a form appended to these rules;
(c) "BIS" means Bureau of Indian Standards;
(d) "CMVR" means Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989;
(e) "HSRP" means High Security Registration Plate;
(f) "ITS" means Intelligent Transport System;
(g) "AIS" means Automotive Industry Standards.

## CHAPTER II — LICENSING

Rule 3. Categories of driving licences
The following categories of motor vehicles are recognised for the purpose of driving licences:
(a) MC 50CC — Motorcycle of engine capacity not exceeding 50cc
(b) MC EX50CC — Motorcycle of engine capacity exceeding 50cc (includes motorcycles, scooters, mopeds above 50cc)
(c) LMV-NT — Light Motor Vehicle - Non-Transport (cars, jeeps, delivery vans up to 7500 kg GVW for personal use)
(d) LMV — Light Motor Vehicle (including transport vehicles up to 7500 kg GVW)
(e) MCWG — Motorcycle with gear
(f) MCWOG — Motorcycle without gear (scooter/scooty/moped with automatic transmission)
(g) MGV — Medium Goods Vehicle (goods vehicle exceeding 7500 kg but not exceeding 12000 kg)
(h) HMV — Heavy Motor Vehicle (exceeding 12000 kg GVW)
(i) HPMV — Heavy Passenger Motor Vehicle (bus carrying more than 12 passengers)
(j) HGV — Heavy Goods Vehicle (goods carriage exceeding 12000 kg GVW)
(k) Adapted Vehicle — Vehicle adapted for persons with disabilities
(l) TRAILER — Trailer attached to a motor vehicle
(m) E-CART — Battery-operated e-cart
(n) E-RICKSHAW — Battery-operated e-rickshaw

Rule 4. Age requirements for driving licences
(a) 16 years — MC 50CC (motorcycle not exceeding 50cc, with gearless transmission)
(b) 18 years — MC EX50CC, MCWG, MCWOG, LMV-NT, LMV
(c) 20 years — Transport vehicles (MGV, HMV, HPMV, HGV) — must have held LMV licence for at least 1 year
(d) 18 years — E-rickshaw, E-cart

Rule 5. Application for learner's licence
(1) Application shall be made in Form 1 (combined application form as per 2019 amendment).
(2) The applicant shall produce following documents:
    (a) Proof of age: Birth certificate, School leaving certificate, Passport, or Aadhaar card
    (b) Proof of address: Aadhaar card, Voter ID, Passport, Utility bill, Ration card, or Bank passbook
    (c) Medical certificate in Form 1-A (for transport vehicle) from a registered medical practitioner
    (d) Proof of training from accredited driving school (for transport vehicle applicants)
    (e) 6 passport-size photographs
    (f) Blood group certificate
(3) Application fee as prescribed by state government.

Rule 6. Learner's licence test
(1) The licensing authority shall conduct a test of competence to drive.
(2) The test for learner's licence shall be on knowledge of:
    (a) Traffic signs and road markings
    (b) Traffic signals
    (c) Rules of the road as contained in Section 118
    (d) Duties of a driver in case of accident
    (e) General awareness of motor vehicle mechanisms
(3) The test may be conducted electronically/online as prescribed.

Rule 7. Validity of learner's licence
(1) A learner's licence shall be valid for a period of six months from the date of issue.
(2) A learner's licence holder must be accompanied by a person holding a valid driving licence for the same type of vehicle while driving.
(3) An "L" sign must be displayed prominently on the vehicle.
(4) Learner's licence holders shall not drive between sunset and sunrise.
(5) Learner's licence holders shall not carry passengers (other than the instructor/accompanying licence holder).

Rule 8. Application for driving licence
(1) Application for driving licence shall be made in Form 4 (Form 1 combined after 2019 amendment).
(2) The applicant must have held a learner's licence for at least 30 days before applying.
(3) For transport vehicles: the applicant must have held an LMV licence for at least 1 year and produce a medical certificate in Form 1-A.

Rule 9. Driving test for obtaining licence
(1) The driving test shall assess:
    (a) Starting the vehicle on a gradient
    (b) Reversing around a corner
    (c) Driving through a figure-of-eight
    (d) Stopping the vehicle at a required point
    (e) Overtaking, turning right and left
    (f) Traffic judgment at intersections
    (g) Proper use of rear-view mirrors
    (h) Knowledge of road rules and signs
(2) For two-wheelers, the test includes:
    (a) Riding in a figure-of-eight
    (b) Balance at slow speed
    (c) Emergency braking

Rule 10. Driving licence validity and renewal
(1) Driving licence is valid for 20 years from the date of issue or until the holder attains the age of 40, whichever is later.
(2) Driving licence issued to persons above 40 years is valid for 10 years.
(3) For transport vehicle drivers, licence is valid for 3 years and must be renewed with a medical certificate.
(4) Application for renewal must be made within 30 days before expiry or within one year after expiry (with late fee).
(5) After one year of expiry, re-test may be required.

Rule 11. International Driving Permit (IDP)
(1) Any person holding a valid Indian driving licence may apply for an IDP.
(2) IDP is valid for one year from date of issue.
(3) IDP is valid for driving in countries that are signatories to the Geneva Convention on Road Traffic 1949 or Vienna Convention on Road Traffic 1968.
(4) Application fee as prescribed.

## CHAPTER III — REGISTRATION OF MOTOR VEHICLES

Rule 47. Application for registration
(1) Application shall be made in Form 20 to the registering authority within whose jurisdiction the applicant resides.
(2) Documents required:
    (a) Sale certificate / invoice from the dealer
    (b) Insurance certificate (mandatory third-party insurance)
    (c) Road tax payment receipt
    (d) Proof of address (Aadhaar, passport, voter ID, etc.)
    (e) PAN card or Form 60 (for vehicles costing more than Rs 10 lakhs)
    (f) Temporary registration certificate (if obtained)
    (g) Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate
    (h) Form 22 — Roadworthiness certificate from manufacturer

Rule 48. Assignment of registration mark
(1) Registration mark shall be in the format: State Code + RTO Code + Series + Number
    Example: MH 01 AB 1234 (Maharashtra, RTO 01, Series AB, Number 1234)
(2) High Security Registration Plates (HSRP) are mandatory for all new vehicles.
(3) HSRP features:
    (a) Chromium-based hologram (hot stamped)
    (b) Laser-etched permanent identification number
    (c) Retro-reflective sheeting (white for front, yellow for rear)
    (d) Snap-lock fittings (non-removable, non-reusable)
    (e) ISD sticker (Inland Letter Sticker) with registration number in text and barcode

Rule 49. Registration certificate (RC)
(1) Registration certificate shall be issued in Form 23 (smart card format).
(2) RC shall contain: Owner details, vehicle details, registration number, engine/chassis number, fuel type, seating capacity, GVW (for commercial vehicles), insurance validity, hypothecation details (if any).
(3) RC is valid for 15 years from date of registration for non-transport vehicles.
(4) For transport vehicles, RC is valid for the period of fitness certificate.

Rule 50. Transfer of ownership
(1) Both transferor and transferee must report to RTO within 14 days.
(2) Transferor shall report transfer in Form 29.
(3) Transferee shall apply for transfer in Form 30.
(4) Documents required:
    (a) Form 29 and Form 30 (signed by both parties)
    (b) Original RC
    (c) Valid insurance certificate
    (d) Valid PUC certificate
    (e) No objection certificate from financier (if vehicle under loan)
    (f) Address proof of transferee
    (g) Identity proof of both parties
(5) Transfer fee as prescribed by state government.
(6) Road tax difference (if applicable, for inter-state transfers).

Rule 51. No Objection Certificate (NOC) for inter-state transfer
(1) NOC must be obtained from the original registering authority before re-registering in another state.
(2) Application in Form 28.
(3) NOC is valid for six months from date of issue.
(4) The vehicle must be re-registered in the new state within the NOC validity period.
(5) Road tax of the original state may be refunded pro-rata (varies by state policy).
(6) Re-registration shall be done with new state's registration mark.

Rule 52. Duplicate RC
(1) If RC is lost, stolen, or mutilated, application for duplicate RC in Form 26.
(2) FIR copy required for lost/stolen RC.
(3) Advertisement in local newspaper regarding loss of RC.
(4) Duplicate RC shall be issued within 30 days.

Rule 53. Fitness certificate for transport vehicles
(1) All transport vehicles must obtain a fitness certificate before being put on road.
(2) Fitness test includes: Brakes, steering, suspension, tyres, lights, horn, wipers, mirrors, exhaust emissions, body condition.
(3) Fitness certificate valid for:
    (a) 2 years from date of first registration
    (b) 1 year for subsequent renewals
(4) Automated fitness testing centres to be established as per 2019 amendment.

## CHAPTER IV — CONSTRUCTION, EQUIPMENT AND MAINTENANCE

Rule 93. Safety features mandatory for all motor vehicles
(1) Every motor vehicle shall be fitted with:
    (a) Head lamps (at least two for four-wheelers, one for two-wheelers)
    (b) Tail lamp and stop lamp
    (c) Direction indicators (turn signals)
    (d) Rear-view mirrors (at least two for four-wheelers, one for two-wheelers)
    (e) Horn (not multi-toned or harsh/shrill)
    (f) Windshield wiper (for vehicles with windshield)
    (g) Speedometer
    (h) Parking lights
    (i) Number plate illumination lamp
    (j) Hazard warning lights
    (k) Reverse lamp (for four-wheelers)
    (l) High-mounted stop lamp (for vehicles manufactured after 2019)
    (m) Anti-lock braking system (ABS) for all vehicles as per AIS 128
    (n) Airbags — minimum 2 front airbags mandatory (from 2022); 6 airbags mandatory (from October 2023)
    (o) Seat belts for all occupants
    (p) Reverse parking sensors/camera (for vehicles manufactured after 2022)

Rule 94. Emission standards
(1) All vehicles must comply with Bharat Stage VI (BS-VI) emission norms from April 1, 2020.
(2) PUC certificate is mandatory:
    (a) For new vehicles: after 1 year from date of registration
    (b) Validity: 6 months
    (c) PUC test centres must be authorised by the transport department
(3) Electric vehicles are exempt from PUC requirements.
(4) PUC limits (BS-VI):
    Petrol vehicles: CO ≤ 0.3%, HC ≤ 200 ppm
    Diesel vehicles: Smoke opacity ≤ 50 HSU
(5) Failure to carry valid PUC: Fine of Rs 10,000 (first offence), Rs 10,000 (repeat) under Section 190(2)

Rule 95. Noise limits
(1) Maximum noise levels:
    (a) Two-wheelers: 80 dB(A)
    (b) Three-wheelers: 77 dB(A)
    (c) Passenger cars: 74 dB(A)
    (d) Heavy vehicles (above 12 tonnes): 77 dB(A)
(2) Modification of silencer/exhaust to increase noise is prohibited.
(3) Pressure horns are prohibited in city/residential areas.

Rule 96. Window tinting / sun film
(1) Visual Light Transmission (VLT) requirements:
    (a) Front windshield: Minimum 70% VLT
    (b) Front side windows: Minimum 50% VLT
    (c) Rear windshield and rear side windows: No restriction after Supreme Court order relaxation
(2) Application of dark films that reduce VLT below prescribed limits is an offence.
(3) Penalty: Vehicle can be impounded; film to be removed on the spot.

Rule 97. Number plate specifications
(1) All vehicles must display HSRP (High Security Registration Plates).
(2) Front plate: White background with black lettering
(3) Rear plate: Yellow background with black lettering (for personal vehicles); White background (for government vehicles)
(4) Font: As per Rule 50 of CMVR — standardised Indian font
(5) Size: 500mm × 120mm for four-wheelers; 200mm × 100mm for two-wheelers
(6) Fancy/stylised number plates are illegal.

## CHAPTER V — CONTROL OF TRANSPORT VEHICLES

Rule 82. Permits for transport vehicles
(1) Types of permits:
    (a) Stage carriage permit — for buses on fixed routes
    (b) Contract carriage permit — for taxis, tourist vehicles
    (c) Goods carriage permit — for trucks, lorries
    (d) Private service vehicle permit — for company buses, school buses
    (e) All India Tourist Permit (AITP) — valid across all states
    (f) National Permit — for goods vehicles operating in more than one state

Rule 83. National Permit for goods vehicles
(1) Application in Form 48 to the home state transport authority.
(2) National Permit allows operation across India.
(3) Fee: As prescribed (varies by state; annual fee + per-state authorisation fee).
(4) National Permit vehicle must comply with fitness requirements of all transiting states.
(5) Composite fee structure introduced under 2019 amendment to eliminate state-by-state fees.

Rule 84. Taxi / Auto-rickshaw permits
(1) Contract carriage permits for taxis and auto-rickshaws issued by Regional Transport Authority.
(2) Meter calibration is mandatory:
    (a) Electronic digital meters (mandatory in most cities)
    (b) Calibration certificate from authorised agency
    (c) Fare card displaying fare chart must be displayed inside the vehicle
(3) Maximum permissible fare as fixed by state/city transport authority.
(4) Refusal to ply or overcharging is an offence under Section 178.
(5) Ride-hailing aggregators (Ola, Uber, etc.) must obtain licence under Section 93 of amended Act.
    (a) Aggregator licence fee: As prescribed by state
    (b) Aggregators must comply with IT Act requirements
    (c) Must share ride data with government (as per IT rules)
    (d) Penalty for aggregator violations: Rs 25,000 (first), up to Rs 1,00,000 (repeat) — Section 193

## CHAPTER VI — INSURANCE

Rule 140. Compulsory third-party insurance
(1) No motor vehicle shall be used in a public place unless a valid insurance policy is in force (Section 146, MV Act).
(2) Types of motor insurance:
    (a) Third-party liability insurance (mandatory)
    (b) Comprehensive/own damage insurance (optional but recommended)
    (c) Personal accident cover for owner-driver (mandatory, minimum Rs 15 lakhs)
(3) Third-party premium rates are fixed by IRDAI annually.
(4) Insurance must cover:
    (a) Death or bodily injury to third party
    (b) Damage to third-party property (limit: Rs 7.5 lakhs as per IRDAI)
(5) Penalty for no insurance: Rs 2,000 (first offence), Rs 4,000 (repeat) + imprisonment up to 3 months — Section 196

Rule 141. Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT)
(1) Claims for compensation in motor accident cases to be filed before MACT.
(2) MACT has jurisdiction where:
    (a) The accident occurred, or
    (b) The claimant resides, or
    (c) The defendant resides
(3) Application in Form prescribed by High Court rules.
(4) No court fee required for filing MACT claim.
(5) Time limit: As per Limitation Act (generally 2 years from accident, but courts have been liberal).
(6) Compensation calculated based on:
    (a) Age and income of deceased/injured
    (b) Multiplier method (as per Supreme Court in Sarla Verma case)
    (c) Medical expenses (actual and future)
    (d) Loss of income / earning capacity
    (e) Pain and suffering
    (f) Loss of consortium, love and affection
    (g) Funeral expenses (in death cases)

Rule 142. Hit and Run compensation
(1) Under Section 161 of MV Act (amended 2019):
    (a) Rs 2,00,000 for death
    (b) Rs 50,000 for grievous hurt
(2) Compensation paid from Solatium Fund maintained by Central Government.
(3) Claim to be filed with Claims Enquiry Officer appointed by state government.
(4) Time limit: 6 months from date of accident (extendable for sufficient cause).

## CHAPTER VII — ROAD SAFETY AND TRAFFIC MANAGEMENT

Rule 15. Rules of the road (Left-hand traffic)
(1) India follows left-hand traffic system.
(2) Keep to the left side of the road at all times.
(3) Overtake only from the right side.
(4) Give way to vehicles coming from the right at uncontrolled intersections.
(5) Give way to vehicles already in a roundabout.
(6) Pedestrians have right of way at marked crossings.

Rule 16. Speed limits (as notified by states)
General speed limits (unless otherwise posted):
(a) Within city limits:
    - Cars/LMV: 50 km/h
    - Two-wheelers: 40 km/h
    - Auto-rickshaws: 35 km/h
    - Heavy vehicles: 40 km/h
(b) On highways:
    - Cars/LMV: 100 km/h (expressways: 120 km/h)
    - Two-wheelers: 80 km/h
    - Goods vehicles: 60-80 km/h (varies by category)
    - Buses: 80 km/h
(c) In school zones/hospital zones: 25 km/h
(d) In residential areas: 30 km/h
Note: States may fix different speed limits for their jurisdictions.

Rule 17. Mandatory use of safety equipment
(1) Seat belts: Mandatory for driver and all passengers in four-wheelers (Section 194A, 194E)
(2) Helmets: Mandatory for rider and pillion rider of two-wheelers (Section 194B)
    - Helmet must conform to BIS standard IS 4151
    - Visor must not be excessively tinted
    - Sikh persons wearing turban are exempt from helmet requirement (Section 129)
(3) Child safety: Children below 4 years must not ride on motorcycles
    - Child seat mandatory in cars for children below specified age (as per state rules)

Rule 18. Prohibited acts while driving
(1) Using handheld mobile phone / communication device — Section 194D — Fine Rs 5,000
(2) Driving under influence of alcohol (BAC > 30 mg/100 ml blood) — Section 185
(3) Driving under influence of drugs — Section 185
(4) Racing on public roads without permission — Section 189
(5) Driving against one-way traffic
(6) Jumping red signal
(7) Driving on footpath
(8) Parking at no-parking zones
(9) Using vehicle horn in silence zones
(10) Driving with headlights on high beam in city areas at night

Rule 19. Traffic signs and their meanings
(1) Mandatory signs (circular with red border):
    - Stop, Give Way, No Entry, One Way, Speed Limit, No Parking, No Honking, No U-Turn, No Overtaking
(2) Cautionary/Warning signs (triangular with red border):
    - Sharp curve, Steep gradient, Narrow road, School zone, Pedestrian crossing, Speed breaker, Railway crossing
(3) Informatory signs (rectangular, blue/green):
    - Hospital, Petrol pump, Parking, Tourist place, Highway distance markers
(4) Road markings:
    - Continuous white line: No overtaking, no lane changing
    - Broken white line: Overtaking permitted
    - Yellow line (single): No parking
    - Yellow line (double): No parking and no stopping
    - Zebra crossing: Pedestrian right of way

Rule 20. Accident procedures
(1) In case of accident, the driver must:
    (a) Stop the vehicle immediately
    (b) Render first aid to injured persons
    (c) Arrange for medical help / transport injured to nearest hospital
    (d) Report to nearest police station within 24 hours
    (e) Information to insurance company within specified time
(2) Good Samaritan protection (Section 134A):
    (a) Any person who provides emergency care to an accident victim shall not be liable for any civil or criminal action
    (b) Good Samaritan cannot be compelled to appear as witness
    (c) Hospital must provide initial treatment without waiting for police formalities or payment
    (d) Good Samaritan guidelines apply to all hospitals (public and private)
(3) Golden Hour (Section 162):
    (a) Cashless treatment during golden hour (first hour after accident) at any hospital
    (b) MACT compensation scheme covers treatment costs
    (c) State government to establish scheme for golden hour treatment

## CHAPTER VIII — VEHICLE SCRAPPING AND GREEN TAX

Rule 150. Vehicle Scrapping Policy (effective from 2022)
(1) Mandatory fitness testing for:
    (a) Government vehicles older than 15 years
    (b) Private vehicles older than 20 years
    (c) Commercial vehicles older than 15 years
(2) Vehicles failing fitness test must be scrapped at Registered Vehicle Scrapping Facilities (RVSF).
(3) Benefits of voluntary scrapping:
    (a) Scrap value certificate
    (b) Rebate on new vehicle purchase (up to 25% of showroom price)
    (c) Road tax rebate (up to 25%)
    (d) Registration fee waiver for new vehicle
(4) Green tax:
    (a) Transport vehicles older than 8 years: 10-25% of annual road tax
    (b) Personal vehicles older than 15 years: Higher green tax at time of RC renewal
    (c) Vehicles registered in highly polluted cities: Additional green tax

## CHAPTER IX — ELECTRONIC ENFORCEMENT AND E-GOVERNANCE

Rule 167. Electronic monitoring and enforcement
(1) State governments may use electronic devices for traffic enforcement:
    (a) Speed cameras (radar and LIDAR)
    (b) Red light violation cameras
    (c) CCTV surveillance
    (d) Body-worn cameras for traffic police
    (e) Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR)
(2) Evidence from electronic devices is admissible under Section 136(3).
(3) E-challan process:
    (a) Violation captured electronically
    (b) E-challan generated and sent to vehicle owner via SMS/email
    (c) Payment can be made online through state portal or Parivahan portal
    (d) 30-day window for payment (varies by state)
    (e) Failure to pay: Vehicle can be impounded; matter referred to court

Rule 168. Vahan and Sarathi portals
(1) Vahan: National vehicle registration database
    (a) Online vehicle registration
    (b) RC transfer and NOC
    (c) Fancy/choice number allocation
    (d) Duplicate RC application
    (e) Hypothecation addition/termination
(2) Sarathi: National driving licence database
    (a) Online LL/DL application
    (b) DL slot booking for test
    (c) DL renewal
    (d) International Driving Permit application
    (e) Duplicate DL
    (f) Address change in DL

## CHAPTER X — ELECTRIC VEHICLES

Rule 170. Registration of electric vehicles
(1) Electric vehicles shall be registered with green number plates.
(2) Registration mark background: Green with white lettering.
(3) Reduced registration fee for EVs (varies by state; many states offer zero registration fee).
(4) Road tax exemption or reduction for EVs in most states.

Rule 171. EV-specific rules
(1) No PUC certificate required for battery electric vehicles.
(2) Charging infrastructure standards: As per BIS/AIS standards.
(3) Battery disposal: Must be done through authorised recyclers as per E-Waste Management Rules.
(4) Speed governors not required for EVs (speed limited by design).
(5) Permit relaxation for electric transport vehicles in some states.
(6) FAME-II subsidy applicable for eligible EVs (as per FAME scheme guidelines).
