## WEST BENGAL

### West Bengal Motor Vehicles Tax Act, 1979
Road tax: ANNUAL system (different from most states which have one-time/lifetime tax)
- Two-wheelers up to 90cc: Rs 350/year
- Two-wheelers 90-170cc: Rs 500/year
- Two-wheelers above 170cc: Rs 750/year
- Cars up to 900cc: Rs 1,700/year
- Cars 900-1490cc: Rs 2,900/year
- Cars 1490-2000cc: Rs 4,100/year
- Cars above 2000cc: Rs 5,000/year
- Option: Pay quarterly, half-yearly, or annually
- Electric vehicles: Exempted from road tax

### West Bengal-Specific Traffic Rules
IMPORTANT: West Bengal significantly reduced central fine rates after 2019 amendment.
West Bengal reduced fines:
- General offence (177): Rs 500 → Rs 100 first, Rs 300 repeat
- Disobedience (179): Rs 2,000 → Rs 500 first, Rs 1,000 repeat
- Driving without licence (181): Rs 5,000 → Rs 1,000
- Speeding (183): Rs 2,000 → Rs 500 first, Rs 1,500 repeat
- Dangerous driving (184): Rs 5,000 → Rs 1,000
- Drunken driving (185): Rs 10,000 → Rs 2,000 first, Rs 3,000 repeat
- No insurance (194): Rs 2,000 → Rs 500
- No helmet (194B): Rs 1,000 → Rs 200 first, Rs 500 repeat
- Mobile phone (194D): Rs 5,000 → Rs 1,000 first, Rs 2,000 repeat
- No seat belt (194A): Rs 1,000 → Rs 200
Note: West Bengal has among the lowest fines in India

(a) Kolkata-specific rules:
    - Hand-pulled rickshaws: Being phased out but still exist
    - Tram routes: Priority for trams on certain roads
    - Auto-rickshaw: Share-auto system prevalent; meter-based for reserved
    - Yellow taxi: Meter mandatory; minimum fare Rs 30
    - App-based cabs: Growing presence
    - Howrah Bridge: No parking, no stopping; specific lane usage
    - Heavy vehicles: Restricted 8 AM to 8 PM in central Kolkata
(b) E-challan: Active in Kolkata, Siliguri, Durgapur, Asansol
(c) Annual tax payment: Can be paid at KMC (Kolkata Municipal Corporation) or online
(d) Durga Puja traffic: Special traffic arrangements during Durga Puja (September-October)

### West Bengal Transport Corporation (WBTC/NBSTC/SBSTC)
- Multiple corporations for different regions
- Online booking: wbtc.co.in
- Kolkata city: WBTC, private buses, minibuses, trams

## BIHAR

### Bihar Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1994
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 6-8% of vehicle cost (one-time)
- Cars up to 1000cc: 7%
- Cars 1000-1500cc: 8%
- Cars above 1500cc: 9%
- Electric vehicles: Reduced rates under Bihar EV Policy

### Bihar-Specific Traffic Rules
(a) Bihar follows central fine rates with some modifications
(b) Fine structure: Moderate — between central rates and reduced-rate states
(c) Patna-specific:
    - Heavy vehicles restricted during peak hours
    - Mahatma Gandhi Setu: Specific timing and speed restrictions
    - Auto-rickshaw: Fare regulated by district administration
    - E-rickshaws: Rapidly growing — simplified permit process
(d) E-challan: Being rolled out in Patna, Gaya, Muzaffarpur
(e) Sand mining transport: Strict permits, GPS tracking mandatory
(f) Flood season: Road closure and diversion protocols during monsoon

### Bihar State Road Transport Corporation (BSRTC)
- Online booking: bsrtc.bih.nic.in
- Limited premium services; expanding fleet

### Bihar RTO
38+ RTOs
Main RTO: Patna
Website: transport.bih.nic.in

## JHARKHAND

### Jharkhand Motor Vehicles Taxation
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 5-7% (one-time)
- Cars: 6-9% based on engine capacity
- Commercial vehicles: Quarterly/annual tax
- Electric vehicles: Exempted

### Jharkhand-Specific Rules
(a) Follows central fine rates largely
(b) Mining vehicle regulations: Strict rules for coal/mineral transport vehicles
    - Mandatory GPS tracking
    - Route restrictions
    - Weight checks at mining pit-heads and state borders
    - Special permits for oversize mining equipment
(c) E-challan: Active in Ranchi, Jamshedpur, Dhanbad
(d) Tribal area regulations: Special provisions in scheduled areas
(e) National Highway enforcement: Active on NH 33 (Ranchi-Dhanbad)

### Jharkhand RTO
24 RTOs
Main RTO: Ranchi
Website: jhtransport.gov.in

## ODISHA

### Odisha Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1975
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 5-7% (one-time)
- Cars up to 1000cc: 6%
- Cars 1000-1500cc: 7%
- Cars above 1500cc: 8-9%
- Electric vehicles: Exempted under Odisha EV Policy

### Odisha-Specific Traffic Rules
(a) Odisha follows central fine rates with some reductions:
    - No helmet: Rs 1,000 → Rs 500
    - General offence: Rs 500 → Rs 200
(b) Bhubaneswar-specific:
    - Smart city traffic management
    - E-challan: Fully operational
    - ANPR cameras at key intersections
    - Mo Bus (city bus service): Dedicated lanes in some areas
(c) Puri pilgrimage traffic: Special arrangements during Rath Yatra, peak tourist season
(d) Cyclone protocol: Vehicle operation rules during cyclone warnings (Odisha is cyclone-prone)
(e) Coastal road regulations: Special rules during fishing season
(f) Mining transport: Coal and mineral transport regulations similar to Jharkhand

### Odisha State Road Transport Corporation (OSRTC)
- Mo Bus (Bhubaneswar city)
- Online booking available
- Expanding fleet and routes

## MADHYA PRADESH

### MP Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1991
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 5-8% (one-time)
- Cars up to 1000cc: 6%
- Cars 1000-1500cc: 7%
- Cars 1500-2000cc: 8%
- Cars above 2000cc: 10%
- Diesel surcharge: 1-2%
- Electric vehicles: 100% exemption (MP EV Policy)

### MP-Specific Traffic Rules
(a) MP follows central fine rates with some modifications
(b) Bhopal/Indore-specific:
    - E-challan: Fully operational
    - BRTS in Bhopal and Indore
    - Auto-rickshaw: Meter mandatory
    - Heavy vehicle timing restrictions
(c) Tiger reserve areas: Speed limits near Panna, Bandhavgarh, Kanha, Satpura
    - Night driving restrictions in core zones
    - Wildlife crossing signage
(d) E-challan: Active in Bhopal, Indore, Jabalpur, Gwalior
(e) Highway enforcement: NH 46 (Bhopal-Indore), NH 44 (Bhopal–Hoshangabad)
(f) Sand mining transport: Regulated with GPS tracking

### MP State Road Transport Corporation (MPRTC)
- Extensive network
- Online booking: mprtc.gov.in
- Premium services on major routes

### MP RTO
52+ RTOs
Main RTO: Bhopal

## ASSAM

### Assam Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1936
Road tax rates:
- Two-wheelers: 4-6% (one-time/periodic)
- Cars: 5-7%
- Electric vehicles: Reduced rates

### Assam-Specific Traffic Rules
(a) Assam follows central fine rates with some reductions
(b) Guwahati-specific:
    - Heavy vehicle restriction during peak hours
    - Auto-rickshaw: Share-auto system; regulated fares
    - Flood season: Road closure protocols during Brahmaputra flooding
(c) Tea garden area: Special vehicle permits for estate roads
(d) Kaziranga area: Strict speed limits (40 km/h) on NH 37 through Kaziranga National Park
    - Night driving restrictions
    - Wildlife crossing priority
    - Heavy fine for overspeeding through park area
(e) E-challan: Being introduced in Guwahati
(f) Flood protocol: Regular road submersion during monsoon — vehicle damage prevention guidelines

### Assam State Transport Corporation (ASTC)
- Covers all 33 districts
- Online services expanding
- Premium: Limited; improving

## SIKKIM

### Sikkim Motor Vehicles Tax
Road tax: Lower rates (3-5%)

(a) Green protocol: Strict environmental rules

(f) Night driving: Restricted on mountain roads
(g) Plastic-free: No plastic waste in vehicles
(h) Electric vehicles: Actively promoted for tourist transport
