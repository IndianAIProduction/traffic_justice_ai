MOTOR VEHICLES ACT 1988 — COMPREHENSIVE PENALTY SCHEDULE
(As amended by Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019)
Complete Reference for All Offences and Penalties

## PART A — PENALTIES FOR DRIVING AND LICENSING OFFENCES

Section 177 — General offences
Offence: Violation of any provision of the Act, rules, or regulations for which no specific penalty is provided
First offence: Fine up to Rs 500
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 1,500
Imprisonment: None
Note: This is the catch-all provision. States may reduce the amount.

Section 177A — Violation of road regulations
Offence: Contravention of regulations made by the State Government for road use
First offence: Fine up to Rs 500
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 1,500
Note: Covers violations of state-specific road regulations

Section 178 — Travelling without ticket in public transport
Offence: Travelling in a stage carriage without valid ticket, or refusing to pay fare
Fine: Rs 500
Note: Also covers refusal to ply by contract carriage driver

Section 179 — Disobedience of orders, obstruction and refusal to give information
Offence: Disobeying orders of a police officer or authorised person; refusing to give information; giving false information
First offence: Fine up to Rs 2,000
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 4,000
Imprisonment: Up to 6 months, or fine, or both
Note: Includes failing to stop when signalled by police

Section 180 — Allowing unauthorised persons to drive
Offence: Causing or permitting a person who does not hold a valid driving licence to drive
First offence: Fine up to Rs 5,000
Imprisonment: Up to 3 months, or fine, or both
Note: Owner/person in charge is liable

Section 181 — Driving without a valid driving licence
Offence: Driving a motor vehicle without holding an effective driving licence (Section 3/4 contravention)
First offence: Fine up to Rs 5,000
Imprisonment: Up to 3 months, or fine, or both
Note: Includes driving a class of vehicle not covered by the licence held

Section 182 — Driving despite disqualification
Offence: Driving a motor vehicle while disqualified from holding a licence
Fine: Rs 10,000
Imprisonment: Up to 3 months, or fine, or both

Section 182A — Oversize vehicles
Offence: Using a motor vehicle exceeding prescribed size limits
Fine: Minimum Rs 5,000
Note: Added by 2019 amendment

Section 182B — Overloading of passengers in goods vehicle
Offence: Carrying passengers in a goods vehicle beyond permitted number
Fine: Rs 2,000 base + Rs 1,000 per extra passenger
Imprisonment: Up to 1 year for goods vehicle driver
Note: Added by 2019 amendment

## PART B — PENALTIES FOR DANGEROUS AND NEGLIGENT DRIVING

Section 183 — Exceeding speed limit
For Light Motor Vehicles (LMV):
First offence: Fine Rs 2,000
Repeat offence: Fine Rs 4,000
For Medium/Heavy Goods/Passenger Vehicles:
First offence: Fine Rs 4,000
Repeat offence: Fine Rs 8,000
License action: Seizure of licence for repeat offence
Note: Speed to be determined by electronic devices; radar/LIDAR evidence admissible

Section 184 — Dangerous driving
Offence: Driving in a manner dangerous to the public (rash/negligent driving on road)
First offence: Fine up to Rs 5,000; imprisonment up to 6 months; or both
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 10,000; imprisonment up to 2 years; or both
Note: Includes driving on wrong side, zig-zag driving, running red lights recklessly

Section 184A — Dangerous driving resulting in death (NEW — 2019 Amendment)
Offence: Whoever causes death of any person by driving rashly or negligently
Punishment: Imprisonment up to 7 years; fine up to Rs 7 lakhs
License action: Cancellation of driving licence for minimum 2 years
Note: This is a significant new provision. Previously handled under IPC 304A.

Section 185 — Drunken driving / Driving under influence of drugs
Offence: Driving under influence of alcohol (BAC > 30 mg per 100 ml of blood) or drugs
First offence: Fine up to Rs 10,000; imprisonment up to 6 months; or both
Repeat offence (within 3 years): Fine up to Rs 15,000; imprisonment up to 2 years; or both
License action: Licence may be suspended/cancelled
BAC testing: Breathalyser or blood test; refusal to undergo test is an offence under Section 203
Note: Zero tolerance for commercial/transport vehicle drivers in many states

Section 186 — Driving when mentally or physically unfit
Offence: Driving when suffering from a disease or disability that makes driving dangerous
First offence: Fine up to Rs 1,000
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 2,000
License action: Licence may be revoked

Section 187 — Offences relating to accidents — hit and run
Offence: Failing to stop after an accident, failing to report, or fleeing the scene
For simple injury: Fine up to Rs 5,000; imprisonment up to 6 months
For grievous hurt: Imprisonment up to 1 year; fine; or both
For death: Imprisonment up to 2 years; fine; or both
Hit-and-run compensation (Section 161): Rs 2,00,000 (death); Rs 50,000 (grievous hurt)
Note: Driver must stop, render assistance, report to police within 24 hours

Section 188 — Endangerment of personal safety of others
Offence: Driving in such a way as to cause physical harm to others through negligence
Punishment: As per provisions of IPC also applicable

Section 189 — Racing on public roads
Offence: Racing or conducting speed trials without written permission of state government
First offence: Fine up to Rs 5,000; imprisonment up to 1 month; or both
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 10,000; imprisonment up to 1 month; or both
Note: Organisers and participants both liable

## PART C — PENALTIES FOR REGISTRATION AND PERMIT OFFENCES

Section 190 — Using vehicle in unsafe condition
Offence: Using a vehicle that does not comply with requirements of the Act / is in unsafe condition
First offence: Fine up to Rs 5,000; imprisonment up to 3 months
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 10,000
Note: Includes driving without valid PUC certificate

Section 191 — Offences by drivers of vehicles transporting dangerous goods
Offence: Not complying with safety requirements for hazardous goods transport
Fine: Rs 3,000 (first); Rs 5,000 (repeat)
Imprisonment: Up to 1 year for serious violations

Section 192 — Using vehicle without registration
Offence: Driving/allowing use of unregistered vehicle in public place
First offence: Fine up to Rs 5,000; imprisonment up to 1 year; or both
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 10,000; imprisonment up to 1 year; or both
Note: Includes displaying false registration mark

Section 192A — Using vehicle without valid permit
Offence: Using a transport vehicle without the permit required under Chapter V
First offence: Fine minimum Rs 5,000, up to Rs 10,000
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 10,000; imprisonment up to 1 year; or both
Note: Vehicle may be impounded

Section 193 — Aggregator violations (NEW — 2019 Amendment)
Offence: Operating as cab aggregator without licence, or violating licence conditions
First offence: Fine up to Rs 25,000
Repeat offence: Fine up to Rs 1,00,000
License action: Cancellation of aggregator licence for repeat offence
Note: Applicable to Ola, Uber, and similar ride-hailing platforms

## PART D — PENALTIES FOR INSURANCE AND SAFETY OFFENCES

Section 194 — Driving without valid insurance
Offence: Using motor vehicle without valid third-party insurance policy
First offence: Fine Rs 2,000; imprisonment up to 3 months; or both
Repeat offence: Fine Rs 4,000; imprisonment up to 3 months; or both
Note: Insurance is mandatory under Section 146. Vehicle can be impounded.

Section 194A — Not wearing seat belt (driver)
Offence: Driver not wearing seat belt while driving
Fine: Rs 1,000
Note: Applicable to all four-wheeler vehicles with seat belts

Section 194B — Riding without helmet (two-wheeler)
Offence: Riding motorcycle / two-wheeler without helmet (applies to both rider and pillion)
Fine: Rs 1,000
License action: 3-month licence disqualification
Note: Helmet must conform to BIS standard IS 4151
Exemption: Sikhs wearing turbans are exempt (Section 129)

Section 194C — Not giving way to emergency vehicles
Offence: Failing to give way to ambulance, fire brigade, or police vehicle on emergency duty
Fine: Rs 10,000
Note: Applies to all road users. Emergency vehicles have absolute right of way.

Section 194D — Using mobile phone while driving
Offence: Using handheld communication device (mobile phone) while driving
Fine: Rs 5,000
Note: Hands-free devices may be permitted but discouraged. Includes texting, browsing, video calls.

Section 194E — Not wearing seat belt (passengers)
Offence: Passengers not wearing seat belts
Fine: Rs 1,000
Note: Driver responsible for ensuring all passengers wear seat belts

Section 194F — Overloading of two-wheelers
Offence: Carrying more than one pillion rider, or carrying a child in unsafe manner
Fine: Rs 2,000
License action: 3-month licence disqualification
Imprisonment: Up to 3 months for repeat offence

Section 196 — Driving transport vehicle without fitness certificate
Offence: Operating transport vehicle without valid fitness certificate
Fine: Minimum Rs 5,000
Note: Vehicle to be taken off road until fitness certificate is obtained

## PART E — PENALTIES FOR SPECIAL OFFENCES

Section 194G — Power to detain vehicles used without licence or registration (NEW)
Offence: Operating vehicle without proper documentation
Action: Vehicle can be detained/impounded by authorised officer
Release: Upon production of valid documents and payment of fine

Section 196A — Power to increase penalties (NEW — 2019 Amendment)
The Central Government may increase the penalty amounts by notification, considering inflation and other factors, up to 10% annually.

Section 198 — Offences by companies
Where an offence is committed by a company, every person in charge of the company shall be deemed guilty.
Penalty: As applicable for the specific offence, plus additional fine up to Rs 1 lakh for the company.

Section 199 — Offences by juveniles
Offence: Minor driving a motor vehicle
Guardian/owner liability: Fine Rs 25,000; imprisonment up to 3 years
Vehicle: Registration of the motor vehicle shall be cancelled
Juvenile: Tried under Juvenile Justice Act
Note: One of the strictest provisions. Owner of the vehicle is jointly liable.

## PART F — COMPOUNDING OF OFFENCES

Section 200 — Compounding of offences
(1) The following offences may be compounded (settled by paying fine without going to court):
    All offences under Sections 177 to 194F (except 184A, 185, 189 in serious cases)
(2) Compounding officer: Police officer not below the rank of Inspector, or Transport officer
(3) Compounding must be done within 60 days of the date of offence
(4) Receipt of compounding to be issued
(5) No further prosecution after valid compounding

Section 201 — Power to arrest without warrant
(1) A police officer may arrest without warrant any person who commits an offence under:
    Section 184 (dangerous driving)
    Section 185 (drunken driving)
    Section 189 (racing)
(2) Person arrested shall be produced before Magistrate within 24 hours.

Section 203 — Refusal to submit to breath/blood test
Offence: Refusing to undergo alcohol/drug test when required by police officer
Presumption: Refusal creates presumption that blood alcohol level exceeds permitted limit
Note: This effectively means refusal is treated as an offence equivalent to Section 185

Section 206 — Power to impound documents and vehicle
(1) Any police officer or transport authority officer may demand production of:
    (a) Driving licence
    (b) Registration certificate
    (c) Insurance certificate
    (d) PUC certificate
    (e) Permit (for transport vehicles)
    (f) Fitness certificate (for transport vehicles)
(2) Failure to produce: Vehicle may be impounded
(3) Documents must be produced within 15 days at the specified police station/RTO

Section 207 — Power to detain vehicles
(1) A police officer may detain/seize a motor vehicle if there is reason to believe:
    (a) The vehicle is being used without registration
    (b) Without valid insurance
    (c) Without valid permit
    (d) In commission of a cognisable offence
(2) Detained vehicle to be released upon:
    (a) Production of valid documents
    (b) Payment of penalty/fine
    (c) Court order
