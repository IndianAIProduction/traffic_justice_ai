VEHICLE REGISTRATION RULES AND PROCEDURES — COMPREHENSIVE GUIDE
(Based on Motor Vehicles Act 1988, CMVR 1989, and State-specific Rules)
Applicable Across All Indian States and Union Territories

## PART A — NEW VEHICLE REGISTRATION

### Step 1: Purchase and Temporary Registration
(1) Upon purchasing a new vehicle from an authorised dealer:
    (a) Dealer provides: Sale invoice, Form 20 (application for registration), Form 21 (sale certificate), Form 22 (roadworthiness certificate from manufacturer)
    (b) Dealer arranges temporary registration (valid for 1 month)
    (c) Temporary registration plate: White background with red lettering, format: "TR" + dealer code + serial number
    (d) Temporary registration allows transit from dealer to owner's address and to RTO

### Step 2: Documents Required for Registration
Mandatory documents:
1. Form 20 — Application for registration (filled by owner)
2. Form 21 — Sale certificate from dealer/manufacturer
3. Form 22 — Roadworthiness certificate from manufacturer
4. Invoice/Sale receipt (original)
5. Insurance certificate (minimum third-party; comprehensive recommended)
6. PAN card or Form 60/61 (mandatory for vehicles costing more than Rs 10 lakhs)
7. Address proof (Aadhaar card, passport, voter ID, utility bill)
8. Identity proof (Aadhaar, PAN, passport)
9. Road tax payment receipt
10. Passport-size photographs (2-4 copies)
11. HSRP order confirmation (High Security Registration Plate)
12. Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate — for BS-VI vehicles, first PUC after 1 year

Additional for transport vehicles:
13. Fitness certificate
14. Permit (if applicable)
15. Goods Tax payment receipt (for goods vehicles in applicable states)

### Step 3: Registration Process
Online (via Vahan portal — vahan.parivahan.gov.in):
1. Dealer initiates online registration through Vahan dealer module
2. Owner's details entered and documents uploaded
3. Road tax calculated and paid online
4. RTO slot booked for vehicle inspection (if required)
5. Registration number assigned
6. HSRP ordered and fitted
7. Smart card RC dispatched

Offline:
1. Visit RTO with all documents
2. Submit forms and pay fees at counter
3. Vehicle inspection at RTO (verification of chassis/engine number)
4. Registration number assigned
5. RC issued (smart card sent by post; acknowledgement given immediately)

### Step 4: Registration Charges (Approximate)
Component | Amount
Registration fee (two-wheeler) | Rs 300-600
Registration fee (four-wheeler) | Rs 600-1,500
Road tax (varies by state) | 2-20% of vehicle value
HSRP charges (two-wheeler) | Rs 400-600
HSRP charges (four-wheeler) | Rs 600-1,100
Smart card RC fee | Rs 200
Hypothecation endorsement | Rs 500 (if vehicle on loan)
Total (approximate for car): Rs 5,000-15,000 + road tax

## PART B — ROAD TAX BY STATE

Road tax is the largest component of registration cost. It varies significantly by state.

### Road Tax Rates (Personal Four-Wheeler — Approximate)

Delhi: 4-12.5% of vehicle cost (based on price slab)
- Up to Rs 6 lakhs: 4%
- Rs 6-10 lakhs: 7%
- Above Rs 10 lakhs: 10%
- Diesel vehicles: Additional 2.5% surcharge

Maharashtra: Lifetime road tax based on vehicle cost and fuel type
- Petrol car up to Rs 10 lakhs: ~10-11%
- Petrol car above Rs 10 lakhs: ~12-13%
- Diesel vehicles: Additional 2-3%
- EV: Exempted or nominal (Rs 500-2,000)

Karnataka: Lifetime road tax
- Up to Rs 5 lakhs: 13%
- Rs 5-10 lakhs: 14%
- Rs 10-20 lakhs: 17%
- Above Rs 20 lakhs: 18%

Tamil Nadu: Lifetime road tax
- Up to Rs 5 lakhs: ~6%
- Rs 5-10 lakhs: ~8%
- Above Rs 10 lakhs: ~10-12%

Uttar Pradesh: Lifetime road tax
- Petrol vehicles: 8-10% based on engine capacity
- Diesel vehicles: 8-10% + surcharge
- CNG/LPG vehicles: Reduced rate

Gujarat: One-time road tax
- Two-wheelers: 6% for first 7 years
- Four-wheelers: 6-7%
- Higher rates for luxury vehicles

Kerala: Lifetime road tax
- Motor cars: 6% of purchase price
- Larger vehicles: Higher rates

Rajasthan: Lifetime / periodic road tax
- Small cars (up to 800cc): 5%
- Medium cars: 6-8%
- Large cars/SUVs: 8-10%

West Bengal: Annual road tax
- Two-wheelers: Rs 350-750 per year
- Cars: Rs 1,000-5,000 per year based on engine capacity
- Can be paid quarterly/annually

Andhra Pradesh and Telangana: Lifetime road tax
- Petrol cars: 12-14%
- Diesel cars: 14-16%
- EVs: Exempted until 2025

Punjab and Haryana: Lifetime road tax
- Cars: 6-9% based on vehicle value
- Two-wheelers: 5-7%

Note: Road tax rates change frequently. Check state transport website for current rates.

### Two-Wheeler Road Tax (Approximate Range)
Most states: 4-12% of vehicle value (lifetime tax)
Some states (West Bengal): Annual tax Rs 350-750

### Electric Vehicle Road Tax Exemptions
Many states offer full or partial road tax exemption for EVs:
- Delhi: Road tax exemption for battery EVs
- Maharashtra: Exemption for first 5 years
- Gujarat: Road tax exemption
- Karnataka: Exemption for EVs
- Telangana: 100% exemption until 2025
- Uttar Pradesh: Exemption for first 5 years
- Rajasthan: 100% exemption

## PART C — TRANSFER OF VEHICLE OWNERSHIP

### When Required
- Sale of vehicle to another person
- Gift/inheritance of vehicle
- Transfer from deceased owner's name to legal heir
- Transfer between family members

### Documents Required
From seller (transferor):
1. Form 29 — Notice of transfer of ownership (signed by seller)
2. Form 30 — Application for transfer (signed by both)
3. Original RC (Registration Certificate)
4. Valid insurance certificate
5. Valid PUC certificate
6. NOC from financier (if vehicle under loan/hypothecation)
7. ID proof of seller
8. Delivery note from seller

From buyer (transferee):
1. Form 30 — signed by buyer
2. Address proof
3. ID proof
4. Passport-size photographs
5. New insurance in buyer's name (or endorsement of existing policy)

### Process
1. Both parties sign Form 29 and Form 30
2. Within 14 days, transferor intimates RTO of transfer (Form 29)
3. Transferee applies to RTO for transfer (Form 30) within 14 days
4. Transferee pays transfer fee (Rs 500-2,000 depending on vehicle type and state)
5. RTO updates records and issues new RC in transferee's name (or endorsement on existing RC)

### Transfer Fee
Two-wheeler: Rs 300-500
Four-wheeler (non-transport): Rs 500-1,500
Commercial vehicles: Rs 1,000-3,000
Note: Varies by state

### Important Notes
- Failure to intimate transfer within 14 days: Fine up to Rs 5,000
- Seller remains liable for violations committed with the vehicle until transfer is recorded
- Insurance must be transferred or new insurance obtained
- If transferee is in a different state, NOC required for inter-state transfer

## PART D — INTER-STATE TRANSFER (NOC)

### When Required
When a vehicle registered in one state is being permanently relocated to another state.

### Procedure
Step 1: Obtain NOC from original state
- Apply at the RTO where vehicle is registered
- Submit: Application in Form 28, original RC, address proof of new state, valid insurance, valid PUC, road tax receipt
- RTO issues NOC after verifying no pending dues/challans
- NOC valid for 6 months

Step 2: Re-register in new state
- Visit RTO in new state within 6 months of NOC issuance
- Submit: NOC, original RC, Form 20 (new registration application), address proof, insurance, PUC
- Pay road tax of new state (original state may refund pro-rata — varies)
- New registration number assigned (new state's code)
- HSRP fitted with new number
- New smart card RC issued

### BH (Bharat) Series Registration
Introduced in 2021, BH series eliminates need for re-registration when moving states.
- Eligible: Defence personnel, Central/State government employees, PSU employees, and private sector employees of companies with offices in 4+ states
- Format: YY BH XXXX (e.g., 22 BH 1234 AB)
- Motor vehicle tax: 2-year based (paid every 2 years at 8-12% based on vehicle cost)
- No NOC required for state transfer
- Available via Vahan portal during registration
- Green background BH plates for EVs

## PART E — HYPOTHECATION (LOAN VEHICLES)

### Registration with Hypothecation
When a vehicle is purchased on loan, hypothecation (HP) is endorsed on the RC in the financier's name.
- RC shows: "Hypothecated to [Bank/NBFC name]"
- Owner cannot sell/transfer without financier's NOC

### Termination of Hypothecation
After loan repayment:
1. Obtain Form 35 (NOC for HP termination) from bank/financier
2. Apply to RTO for HP termination
3. Submit: Form 35, original RC, loan closure letter
4. RTO removes HP endorsement from RC
5. Fee: Rs 200-500

### Continuation of Hypothecation
When vehicle is sold with existing loan:
1. Loan transferred to new buyer
2. HP endorsement updated with new owner's name
3. Financier's consent required

## PART F — DUPLICATE / REPLACEMENT RC

### When Needed
- RC lost or stolen
- RC damaged or illegible
- RC details need correction

### Process
1. File FIR (for lost/stolen RC) at nearest police station
2. Publish advertisement in local newspaper stating loss of RC
3. Apply at RTO: Form 26 (application for duplicate RC)
4. Submit: FIR copy, newspaper cutting, affidavit, insurance copy, address proof
5. Fee: Rs 300-500
6. Duplicate RC issued within 30 days

## PART G — FANCY / CHOICE NUMBER

### Availability
Most RTOs offer choice/fancy registration numbers through auction or fixed fee.

### Categories
1. VIP numbers (0001, 0786, 9999, etc.): Auction — Rs 25,000 to Rs 25 lakhs+
2. Semi-fancy numbers (specific digits): Fixed fee or auction
3. Regular choice: Minor premium over random number

### Process
1. During registration on Vahan portal, select "Choice Number"
2. View available numbers and prices
3. Pay additional fee for selected number
4. Number reserved upon payment

## PART H — VEHICLE FITNESS AND RENEWAL OF REGISTRATION

### Non-Transport Vehicles
- Registration valid for 15 years
- After 15 years: Must pass fitness test for renewal
- Renewal period: 5 years at a time after initial 15 years
- Fitness test: Automated testing at authorised centres
- If vehicle fails fitness: Must repair and re-test, or scrap

### Transport Vehicles
- Initial fitness certificate: Valid for 2 years from registration
- Renewal: Every 1 year thereafter
- Fitness test: Comprehensive — brakes, steering, lights, emissions, body condition
- Automated fitness centres being established nationwide

### Vehicle Scrapping (End of Life)
- Government vehicles: Must be scrapped after 15 years
- Private vehicles: After 20 years if failing fitness test
- Commercial vehicles: After 15 years if failing fitness test
- Benefits of scrapping: Rebate on new vehicle (up to 25% of ex-showroom price)

## PART I — COMMON REGISTRATION PROBLEMS AND SOLUTIONS

### Problem: Dealer delaying registration
Solution: 
1. Dealer must complete registration within 7 days of sale
2. Complain to RTO if delayed
3. File complaint on Vahan portal
4. Consumer court complaint if persistent delay

### Problem: Wrong details on RC
Solution:
1. Apply for correction at RTO with supporting documents
2. No fee if error is RTO's fault
3. Minor correction fee if owner's error

### Problem: Vehicle stolen — what to do
Solution:
1. File FIR immediately at nearest police station
2. Inform RTO in writing
3. Inform insurance company within specified time
4. RTO marks vehicle as stolen in Vahan database
5. Insurance claim for vehicle theft (if comprehensive insurance)

### Problem: Pending challans blocking transfer/NOC
Solution:
1. Pay all pending challans
2. Challans can be paid online through state e-challan portal
3. After payment, re-apply for transfer/NOC

### Problem: Hypothecation not removed after loan closure
Solution:
1. Contact bank for Form 35 / NOC
2. RBI guidelines require banks to issue NOC within 14 days of loan closure
3. If bank delays: File complaint with Banking Ombudsman
4. Apply to RTO with available documents and bank correspondence
